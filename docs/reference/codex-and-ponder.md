# The Chronicle & Ponder

<p class="bb-lead"><em>Bannerbound never hands you a wall of tutorials up front. It teaches you the way a real people learn — one discovery at a time. Everything you figure out is written down, everything urgent is put in front of you, and every crafting station can show you exactly how it works.</em></p>

!!! abstract "The short version"
    - Press **J** to open the **Chronicle** — a book that fills *permanently* as you discover systems, use blocks, research ideas, and survive crises.
    - Entries are sorted into **categories**, some **secret** until you unlock them (use a block, reach an era, finish a research…). You can **search** and **auto-pin** tutorials.
    - The side **Objective Tracker** is separate: it shows only your *live, current* tasks.
    - If the **Create** mod is installed, stations and research nodes offer **"Hold [W] to Ponder"** — animated scenes that build and operate each machine. Without Create, the feature is simply absent.

## The Chronicle — press J

The **Chronicle** is your civilization's book of knowledge: *"A record of what your people have learned."* Press **J** at any time to open it. Its guiding principle, written on the very first page, is **discovery, not homework**.

You are never asked to read ahead. Instead, the Chronicle writes itself as you play. Light your first **Campfire** and a starter entry appears. Knap your first stone tool, form a Bloomery, cross into a new era, weather a starvation crisis — each of these leaves a permanent page behind. Once an entry is written, it stays forever, so the Chronicle doubles as your reference long after the moment that unlocked it has passed.

That permanence is the key distinction to keep in mind:

> The Chronicle keeps the permanent knowledge; the tracker keeps the live task list.

!!! tip "Read it when you're curious, not when you're told"
    New entries announce themselves quietly. There's no penalty for ignoring them in the heat of a build — they'll be waiting under **J** whenever you want the full explanation. Treat the Chronicle as a library you visit, not a checklist you clear.

### How the book is organized

Entries live in **categories** shown down the side of the book. Each has an icon and a sort order, so related knowledge sits together. Both mods contribute categories to one shared shelf — Core's civilization chapters come first, and Antiquity's craft chapters follow beneath them:

=== "Core (the civilization)"

    | Category | What it collects |
    | --- | --- |
    | **Getting Started** | The Chronicle itself and your very first steps |
    | **Settlement** | Founding, territory and claims, outposts, immigration and growth |
    | **Citizens & Jobs** | Needs, jobs, drop-offs, the Foreman's Rod, tools and the tool age |
    | **Housing & Appeal** | Homes, appeal, and what keeps citizens content |
    | **Research & Culture** | Advancing ideas and your cultural identity |
    | **Governance & War** | Chiefdom vs council, votes, suggestions, crises, war and banners |

=== "Antiquity (Stone-to-Bronze Age)"

    | Category | What it collects |
    | --- | --- |
    | **Crafting & Industry** | The knapping, kiln, forge and workshop stations |
    | **Gatherers & Workers** | Foresters, miners, diggers, foragers, and builders |
    | **Hunting & Food** | Farming, fishing, hunting, and feeding your people |
    | **Husbandry** | Animals, herds, fiber and rope |
    | **Settlement & People** | Beds, homes, and early community life |
    | **Defense & Faith** | Weapons, walls, antidotes, and belief |

Click any entry to read it. Pages mix headings, plain text, item showcases, and links that jump straight to related entries — so the Chronicle cross-references itself the same way this wiki does.

### Secret entries unlock as you play

Many entries wait until you've actually met the thing they describe before they're written in. A handful are fully **secret** — hidden from the shelf entirely until you meet their condition, then revealed the moment you earn them. This is what makes the book feel like it's discovering the world alongside you.

An entry can unlock from things like:

- **Using a particular block** for the first time — lighting a Campfire, working a new station
- **Reaching a new era** — advancing your settlement into the next age
- **Obtaining a key item** — your first bone or wooden pickaxe, say
- **Completing a research** or unlocking a cultural idea
- **Hitting a settlement milestone** — a threshold your people cross as they grow

An entry may need **any** one of several of these, or **all** of them together. For example, the **Tools & the Tool Age** entry writes itself the instant you obtain a bone or wooden pickaxe *or* first light a Campfire — whichever happens first. Because these conditions track your actual settlement state, entries you've already earned stay unlocked across sessions.

!!! note "Secret ≠ locked away"
    Secret entries aren't gated content you have to grind for — they're just held back so the book doesn't spoil systems you haven't met yet. Play naturally and the shelf fills on its own.

### Search and auto-pin

Two features keep a growing book usable:

- **Search entries** — a search field filters the whole Chronicle by keyword, so you can jump to "housing" or "bloomery" without scrolling the shelf.
- **Auto-pin tutorials** — a toggle (**Auto-pin tutorials: ON / OFF**) that decides whether freshly-unlocked how-to entries automatically pin themselves to your side tracker. Leave it **on** while you're learning so the next step is always in view; switch it **off** once you know the ropes and want a clean HUD.

## The Objective Tracker — your live task list

Alongside the Chronicle is a small **Objective Tracker** pinned to the side of your screen. Where the Chronicle is a permanent archive of *everything you've ever learned*, the tracker shows only what matters **right now** — the handful of live objectives you're actively working toward.

When you have nothing pending it simply reads *"No current entries."* When a **crisis** fires — starvation being the first most players meet — the tracker shows the path you've chosen to resolve it, step by step, until it's done. Finish the task and it clears itself off the list.

The tracker is a HUD element you can hide or show with its own keybind (**Toggle Journal Tracker**). Auto-pinned tutorials and active objectives both appear here; unpin anything you don't want cluttering the view.

!!! warning "Don't confuse the two"
    A common early mix-up: looking in the Chronicle for a task you swore you saw, or in the tracker for a mechanic you read about earlier. Remember the split — **Chronicle (J) = permanent knowledge**, **Objective Tracker = the live to-do list**. If something vanished from the side of your screen, it wasn't deleted; its permanent entry is still under J.

## Ponder — animated how-to scenes

Some of Bannerbound's crafting stations are hands-on machines with several steps, and reading about them only goes so far. If you have the optional **[Create](optional-mods.md)** mod installed, the modpack borrows Create's beloved **Ponder** system to *show* you instead — a narrated, animated 3D scene that assembles the station and walks through using it.

Wherever a Ponder is available, you'll see the prompt **"Hold [W] to Ponder."** It appears in two places:

- On a **research node** in the research screen, when that idea has a Ponder attached.
- On a **station or item** — hover it in your inventory and hold **W**, exactly as you would for any Create-ponderable block.

Hold the key and Create's Ponder UI opens, dropping you straight into the scene for that station. Each machine typically has two scenes: one for **building** it and one for **operating** it. Here, for instance, is how the **Kiln** ponder reads — *"Right-click Cobblestone with a Clay Ball to make Clayed Cobblestone… Stack eight of them into a 2×2×2 cube… The cube collapses into a Kiln."* Then the operation scene shows loading, lighting, and collecting the fired goods.

### Stations with a Ponder

Every core Antiquity workstation has its own Ponder scene. Each is filed under a Ponder **tag** you can browse, and each links back to the full wiki page for that craft:

| Ponder | What it shows | Full guide |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **The Crafting Stone** | The off-station bench where flint and stone become the first tools | [Knapping](../antiquity/knapping.md) |
| **The Woodworking Table** | Batch-cut logs into planks, stairs and frames | [Woodworking](../antiquity/woodworking.md) |
| **The Mason's Bench** | Batch-dress stone into slabs, stairs, walls and bricks | [Masonry](../antiquity/masonry.md) |
| **The Kiln** | Bake clay, ceramics and lime in a clayed-cobblestone cube | [Pottery](../antiquity/pottery.md) |
| **The Bloomery** | Smelting iron without electricity — the Antiquity-era forge | [Metalworking](../antiquity/metalworking.md) |
| **The Crucible** | Gather a metal charge, melt it in a Bloomery, pour it into moulds | [Metalworking](../antiquity/metalworking.md) |
| **The Stone Anvil** | Cast molten metal in moulds and cold-hammer parts into tools | [Metalworking](../antiquity/metalworking.md) |
| **The Tannery** | Scrape, cure and dry raw hides into leather | [Tannery](../antiquity/tannery.md) |
| **The Mortar & Pestle** | Grind ingredients into dyes, inks and pastes | [Herbalism](../antiquity/herbalism.md) |

??? example "A full Ponder, start to finish: the Bloomery"
    Hold **W** on the Bloomery and the scene builds it in front of you, then teaches the whole cycle:

    1. Stack two Mud Bricks where you want the forge.
    2. Right-click the bottom brick with a Block of Coal to form the **Bloomery**.
    3. Sneak + right-click to swing the door open.
    4. Right-click with your ingredients to load them inside.
    5. Ignite it — the flames will die down after a while.
    6. Hold a **bellows** aimed at the Bloomery to keep the fire going.
    7. When smelting is done, open the door and right-click empty-handed to collect your output.

    Reading that is fine; *watching* it place every block and swing the door makes it click instantly. That's the whole point of Ponder.

!!! info "No Create? No problem"
    Ponder is a bonus, not a requirement. If Create isn't installed, the **"Hold [W] to Ponder"** prompts simply don't appear and nothing breaks — the stations work exactly the same, and the Chronicle still explains every one of them in text. See [Optional Mods](optional-mods.md) for how the modpack degrades gracefully around anything you haven't installed.
