# Glossary

<p class="bb-lead"><em>Every word Bannerbound speaks, gathered in one place. When a term on another page sends you here, this is where you find its short definition — and a link straight to the page that covers it in full.</em></p>

!!! abstract "Quick reference — the ladders your people climb"
    Bannerbound grades a lot of things on fixed ladders. Here are the six you'll meet most; each has its own full entry below.

    | Ladder | Rungs, low :material-arrow-right: high |
    | --- | --- |
    | **Quality** of a crafted good | Crude · Standard · Fine · Masterwork · Perfect · Legendary |
    | **Skill** of a worker | Novice · Apprentice · Journeyman · Veteran · Master |
    | **Appeal / Beauty** of a place | Atrocious … *Bland* (neutral) … Breathtaking *(nine steps)* |
    | **Stage** of a settlement | Hearth · Tribe · Village · Town · City |
    | **Era** of a civilization | Ancient · Classical · Medieval · Renaissance · Industrial · Diesel · Atomic · Modern · Future |
    | **Tool age** of your workers | Bone · Wood · Stone · Copper/Tin · Bronze · Iron |

!!! tip "Finding a term fast"
    Use the **On this page** panel to jump by letter, or hit the search shortcut (++slash++) and type the word. Every entry links to the page that *owns* the mechanic — follow it for the whole story.

---

### A

**Anarchy**
:   The leaderless baseline a settlement lives under before it enacts a code of laws. Citizens self-organize into basic jobs, but you can only *ask* them to switch work — you can't pin jobs or issue orders until you form a government. See [Government & Law](../civilization/government.md).

**Annex**
:   To fold a **captured** enemy settlement or city-state into your own faction as a second settlement of your own. Annexing needs a free settlement slot, unlocked by the **Feudalism** research. See [Diplomacy](../civilization/diplomacy.md).

**Appeal** *(also Beauty)*
:   How pleasant the blocks around a home or [workshop](../civilization/workshops.md) are, graded on a nine-step scale — **Atrocious · Repulsive · Disgusting · Unappealing · Bland · Pleasant · Attractive · Stunning · Breathtaking** (Bland is the neutral middle). Higher appeal lifts happiness and worker output. See [Housing & Appeal](../civilization/housing.md).

**Archery**
:   The research that puts a true bow in the hands of your hunters and defenders, letting them drop game and raiders from range. It builds on **Fletching**. See [Fletching & Archery](../antiquity/fletching-and-archery.md).

**Astrology**
:   One of the two paths a [Faith](../civilization/faith.md) can follow: you draw your gods into the night sky as glowing constellations in **Pantheon Mode**, each granting a quiet, permanent blessing. Its counterpart is the **Totemic** path. See [Faith & the Stars](../civilization/faith.md).

### B

**Banner** *(faction banner, public standard)*
:   The standard your settlement is *bound* to. While it stands within 24 blocks of the [town hall](../civilization/settlements.md), your menus and workers run; if it falls, the settlement goes silent until it's raised again. You design it in the [Heraldry](../civilization/culture-and-heraldry.md) editor, and an enemy can win a war by stealing it — see [Diplomacy](../civilization/diplomacy.md).

**Barbarian**
:   The umbrella name for the wild warbands beyond your border, split into four camps — **Nomads**, the **Tribe**, **Raiders**, and **Marauders** — each with its own temper set by the land it sits in. Most can be bargained with at **parley**; anger one and it **raids** you. See [Barbarians & Raids](../civilization/barbarians.md).

**Bartering**
:   The research that unlocks cross-settlement and city-state **trade** through the town hall's Diplomacy tab. Both sides also need peace and a trading stockpile. See [Research & Eras](../civilization/research-and-eras.md).

**Beauty**
:   A chunk's own scenic rating, on the same **Atrocious → Breathtaking** scale as [appeal](../civilization/housing.md). Claim beautiful land and it generates more culture. See [Territory](../civilization/territory.md).

**Bloomery**
:   The Antiquity-era forge — a small furnace you build from mud bricks and coal, then keep alive with a **bellows** to smelt ore without electricity. See [Metalworking](../antiquity/metalworking.md).

**Brewing**
:   The craft of fermenting drinks at a **Fermentation Trough**, worked by a **Brewer** once you've researched it. See [Brewing](../antiquity/brewing.md).

**Builder**
:   The worker unlocked alongside **Wall Building** whose whole trade is turning your wall blueprint into standing stone, block by block, drawing materials from your stockpiles. See [Walls & Defenses](../civilization/walls.md).

### C

**Campfire**
:   The block you place and light to found a settlement. Once lit it becomes your **Town Hall** — the block that opens every menu. See [Settlements & Growth](../civilization/settlements.md).

**Chief**
:   The single elected leader of a [Chiefdom](../civilization/government.md). Only the Chief may direct research, assign jobs, change policies, negotiate trade, and issue orders — and lean on that power too hard and unrest can depose them in a coup. See [Government & Law](../civilization/government.md).

**Chiefdom**
:   A form of government that concentrates power in one **Chief**. Fast and direct, well suited to a small or solo settlement, with its own signature policy (**Workload Share**). See [Government & Law](../civilization/government.md).

**Chronicle**
:   Your in-game guidebook, opened with ++j++. It fills *permanently* as you discover systems, use blocks, research ideas, and survive crises — "a record of what your people have learned." See [The Chronicle & Ponder](codex-and-ponder.md).

**City** *(stage)*
:   The grand milestone at the top of the growth ladder — a sprawling seat of civilization. See [Settlements & Growth](../civilization/settlements.md).

**City-state**
:   A neutral, unclaimed settlement run by the game rather than a player. Each has a distinct economy (what it **seeks** and **sells**); you can trade with one or conquer it. See [Diplomacy](../civilization/diplomacy.md).

**Compliance**
:   Whether a citizen will *obey* an order, as opposed to how happy they feel. Living in misery erodes compliance, and low-compliance workers refuse job switches or go on strike. See [Citizens & Needs](../civilization/citizens.md).

**Cooking** *(the Kitchen)*
:   Turning raw ingredients into real meals at a **Stone Cooking Pot**, worked by a **Cook** in a Kitchen workshop. Cooked food is what keeps homes and citizens content. See [Cooking & Food](../antiquity/cooking-and-food.md).

**Council**
:   A form of government that spreads power across the online players, settling weighty matters by majority chat-vote. Needs two or more players; its signature policy is **Opinionated Crowd**. See [Government & Law](../civilization/government.md).

**Coup**
:   The overthrow of a Chief. When unrest runs high the people warn they may rise "at dawn," then depose the leader at first light and install a new one. Address the underlying misery before sunrise to keep your seat. See [Government & Law](../civilization/government.md).

**Crafter**
:   The worker who stands at a bound workshop's work block, fulfills its orders, and gains skill over time. Its counterpart is the [Stocker](../civilization/jobs-and-labor.md) who hauls in the ingredients. See [Workshops & Storage](../civilization/workshops.md).

**Crafting Stone**
:   Your first workbench, carved early on: the off-station surface where you assemble bone tools, haft stone heads, and craft the Foreman's Rod. It also anchors a General Crafts [workshop](../civilization/workshops.md). See [Knapping & First Tools](../antiquity/knapping.md).

**Crisis**
:   A settlement-wide emergency that opens a full-screen decision and drags everyone's mood down until it's resolved. The Chief decides (or a council votes); the first one most players meet is **Starvation**. See [Crises](../civilization/crises.md).

**Crucible**
:   The fired-clay vessel you charge with ore and melt in a **Bloomery**, then pour into moulds to cast metal parts. A freshly-melted crucible is scorching — carry it with **tongs**. See [Metalworking](../antiquity/metalworking.md).

**Culture**
:   A resource your settlement generates from every claimed chunk, spent to fuel the **Culture Research** tree, unlock palettes, and earn Heraldry points. Also the "fuel" that draws new immigrants. See [Culture & Heraldry](../civilization/culture-and-heraldry.md).

**Culture Style**
:   The aesthetic your people build by, chosen once at founding (Forest, Desert, Taiga, and so on). It quietly decides which blocks your citizens find beautiful and which they find ugly. See [Culture & Heraldry](../civilization/culture-and-heraldry.md).

### D

**Devotion**
:   The resource a [Faith](../civilization/faith.md) runs on. It trickles in from every believer, pools across the whole faith, and is spent to advance faith research and raise gods into the sky. See [Faith & the Stars](../civilization/faith.md).

**Diplomacy**
:   The town hall tab where you meet other settlements, city-states, and barbarian camps, read each one's stance toward you, and choose to trade, ally, or make war. See [Diplomacy, Trade & War](../civilization/diplomacy.md).

**Doctrine**
:   A culture-tree research that opens a governing option — a **Faith**, a policy path, or a special ability. **Spiritualism**, the doctrine that unlocks Faith, is the one most players reach for first. See [Culture & Heraldry](../civilization/culture-and-heraldry.md).

**Domain**
:   The sphere a faith's god belongs to, set by the colors of the stars in its constellation. The seven domains — **Harvest, War, Kinship, Knowledge, Craft, Journey, Sea** — each grant a different blessing. See [Faith & the Stars](../civilization/faith.md).

**Drop-off**
:   The storage a gatherer deposits what they bring home into. Any claimed chest, basket, barrel, or stockpile works; a worker with no valid drop-off just stands idle. See [Jobs & Labor](../civilization/jobs-and-labor.md).

### E

**Envoy**
:   A lone messenger a [barbarian](../civilization/barbarians.md) camp sends to your town hall once it has discovered you. **Right-click one to parley**; cut one down and relations plummet. See [Barbarians & Raids](../civilization/barbarians.md).

**Era**
:   The broad epoch your settlement belongs to. There are nine, climbed in order — **Ancient, Classical, Medieval, Renaissance, Industrial, Diesel, Atomic, Modern, Future** — each reached by finishing its keystone research. Advancing an era opens higher-tier research and widens your borders. See [Research & Eras](../civilization/research-and-eras.md).

### F

**Faith**
:   A religion your people found — the first thing in your world bigger than any single banner, able to spread across many settlements. It runs on [Devotion](../civilization/faith.md) and follows either the **Astrology** or **Totemic** path. See [Faith & the Stars](../civilization/faith.md).

**Fletching** *(& Archery)*
:   The craft of making arrows and simple ranged weapons at a **Fletching Station**, refined through a *stretch* timing minigame; the linked **Archery** research adds the bow. See [Fletching & Archery](../antiquity/fletching-and-archery.md).

**Foreman's Rod**
:   The crafted tool that turns menu decisions into real places in the world — marking fields, pens, work regions, drop-offs, and workstations with your clicks. See [Jobs & Labor](../civilization/jobs-and-labor.md).

### G

**Government**
:   How a settlement rules itself — **Anarchy** at first, then a **Chiefdom** or **Council** once you enact a code of laws. Your choice gates which doctrines and policies you can adopt. See [Government & Law](../civilization/government.md).

**Guard**
:   A trained watch job that patrols your borders and fights raiders and hostile mobs — far steadier in a brawl than an ordinary citizen, and it won't be lured off into the wild. See [Jobs & Labor](../civilization/jobs-and-labor.md).

### H

**Haft**
:   The plain wooden handle — almost always a stick — that a knapped or cast tool **head** must be lashed to before it becomes a usable tool (*"You need a stick to haft this."*). See [Tool Tiers & Ages](../antiquity/tool-tiers.md).

**Happiness**
:   A citizen's overall mood, averaged from four equal pillars — **Food, Culture, Comfort, Society** — and landing in a **Content**, **Indifferent**, or **Miserable** band that changes how fast they move, work, and learn. See [Citizens & Needs](../civilization/citizens.md).

**Hearth** *(stage)*
:   Your founding camp — a tiny band of settlers around the fire, before any government. You leave the Hearth stage the moment you enact a code of laws and become a Tribe. See [Settlements & Growth](../civilization/settlements.md).

**Heraldry**
:   The culture research that opens the **Faction Banner** editor, where you layer patterns over a base color. Each layer costs a **Heraldry point** (earned from culture research), and the banner's three most-shown colors become your settlement's menu colors. See [Culture & Heraldry](../civilization/culture-and-heraldry.md).

**Herbalism**
:   The craft of grinding plants into poisons, antidotes, dyes, and pastes at a **Mortar & Pestle** — and the blowdarts that arm your defenders against a raid. See [Herbalism](../antiquity/herbalism.md).

### I

**Immigration**
:   The arrival of newcomers who join your settlement on their own, drawn by culture and gated by a food surplus. Immigration tops you up to each era's **immigration floor**; past that, growth comes from births. See [Settlements & Growth](../civilization/settlements.md).

**Insight**
:   A burst of research progress earned by *doing* — chop a stack of logs and Woodworking surges, mine copper and Metal Working leaps. An Insight shoves the active research forward, sometimes finishing it outright. See [Research & Eras](../civilization/research-and-eras.md).

### J

**Job**
:   The trade a citizen holds — a gatherer role like Forester or Miner, or a workshop role like Crafter and Stocker — set from a citizen's Job tab and balanced from the **Labor board**. Under anarchy you can only *ask* a citizen to switch. See [Jobs & Labor](../civilization/jobs-and-labor.md).

### K

**Kiln**
:   A furnace you form from a cube of clayed cobblestone to fire clay, ceramics, and lime — the first true furnace, long before metal. A Pottery workshop needs one inside it. See [Pottery](../antiquity/pottery.md).

**Knapping**
:   Shaping stone by hand — striking flint against a hard surface to make blades, and working tool heads in a timing minigame. The **Knapping** research advances you into the Stone Age. See [Knapping & First Tools](../antiquity/knapping.md).

### L

**Labor board**
:   The town hall's **Labor** tab, where you set job priorities and per-job caps and let the settlement balance its own workforce — or switch off **Auto-assign** to hand-pick every worker. See [Jobs & Labor](../civilization/jobs-and-labor.md).

**Lexicon**
:   Your settlement's living dictionary of the words it coins as it grows and ages, browsed from the town hall's **Lexicon** tab. The same tongue styles your citizens' given names and trade surnames. See [Culture & Heraldry](../civilization/culture-and-heraldry.md).

### M

**Marauder**
:   The always-hostile plains-and-forest [barbarian](../civilization/barbarians.md) camp — and the namesake of the whole "Barbarian" umbrella. Paying its tribute only buys time; the one lasting answer is to march out and **clear the camp**. See [Barbarians & Raids](../civilization/barbarians.md).

**Masonry**
:   The stone-dressing craft: chisel rough stone into slabs, stairs, walls, bricks, and polished blocks at a **Mason's Bench**, worked by a **Mason**. See [Masonry](../antiquity/masonry.md).

**Metalworking**
:   The craft chain that smelts ore in a **Bloomery**, casts it from a **Crucible** into moulds, and cold-hammers the parts into finished tools on a **Stone Anvil**, worked by a **Smith**. It unlocks the Copper Age. See [Metalworking](../antiquity/metalworking.md).

### O

**Objective Tracker**
:   The small HUD list pinned to the side of your screen showing only your *live* current tasks — auto-pinned tutorials and active crisis steps. It's the counterpart to the permanent [Chronicle](codex-and-ponder.md). See [The Chronicle & Ponder](codex-and-ponder.md).

**Outpost**
:   A lone banner planted *beyond* your border to work one distant deposit — an ore vein or a livestock herd — without dragging your whole territory out to it. It is **not** protected, so guard the road home. See [Territory & Outposts](../civilization/territory.md).

### P

**Palette**
:   A research-unlocked set of blocks your people have learned to love; while active, every block in it grants an appeal bonus wherever it's placed. Palettes fill limited slots and are switched on by a settlement vote, like a policy. See [Culture & Heraldry](../civilization/culture-and-heraldry.md).

**Pantheon**
:   The roster of gods you've drawn into the night sky as glowing constellations, each filling a slot and blessing your settlement. You raise gods in **Pantheon Mode** and expand the roster through faith research. See [Faith & the Stars](../civilization/faith.md).

**Parley**
:   The negotiation you open by right-clicking a barbarian **envoy**: pay tribute, trade, or refuse, bargaining across a **barter** screen where every good has a **worth**. The camp deals once your offer's worth meets its asking value. See [Barbarians & Raids](../civilization/barbarians.md).

**Pioneering spirit**
:   The foraging food trickle that sustains a brand-new **Hearth** — your founding settlers gather to grow the camp before any field is plowed, keeping you in surplus so culture can build. See [Settlements & Growth](../civilization/settlements.md).

**Policy**
:   A standing rule that reshapes how your settlement works (longer shifts, guarded nights, faster roads). Policies fill limited slots, are unlocked by research, and almost every one carries a happiness or compliance cost. See [Government & Law](../civilization/government.md).

**Ponder**
:   An animated, narrated 3D how-to scene for a station or research node, shown when the optional **Create** mod is installed — look for **"Hold [W] to Ponder."** See [The Chronicle & Ponder](codex-and-ponder.md).

**Pottery**
:   The craft of shaping and firing clay into pots, buckets, crucibles, and mud bricks at a **Pottery Slab** (with a **Kiln** to fire them), worked by a **Potter**. See [Pottery](../antiquity/pottery.md).

**Public standard**
:   Another name for your faction [Banner](../civilization/culture-and-heraldry.md) — the standard whose fall silences your settlement and whose capture decides a war. See [Diplomacy](../civilization/diplomacy.md).

### Q

**Quality**
:   The grade a skilled craft comes out at, climbing the ladder **Crude → Standard → Fine → Masterwork → Perfect → Legendary**. It rises with the crafter's skill, the gear in their hands, and how well any crafting minigame is played. See [Workshops & Storage](../civilization/workshops.md).

### R

**Raid**
:   A barbarian warband that spawns in a ring around your settlement and marches on your town hall. Your **Guards** hold the line (the **Night Watch** policy keeps them on the border after dark); repel one and the camp returns larger and sooner. See [Barbarians & Raids](../civilization/barbarians.md).

**Rally** *(Rally Citizens)*
:   The wartime toggle that has your workers down tools and muster to defend the settlement. It only works during an active war or a raid — your people won't rally in peacetime. See [Diplomacy](../civilization/diplomacy.md).

**Raze**
:   To tear a captured settlement, city-state, or barbarian camp down for good, ending its threat permanently. See [Diplomacy](../civilization/diplomacy.md) and [Barbarians & Raids](../civilization/barbarians.md).

**Regent**
:   A stand-in who fills the Chief's role while the Chief is logged off, and steps back down when they return. See [Government & Law](../civilization/government.md).

**Registration Tablet & Paper**
:   The documents you issue at the town hall to invite another player into your settlement — a **Tablet** for the full recruit, a **Paper** as a lighter invitation. Each era you advance raises how many you can issue at once. See [Settlements & Growth](../civilization/settlements.md).

**Resentment**
:   A citizen's personal grievance toward whoever leads them, distinct from general unhappiness. It deepens under neglect (homelessness is the classic trigger) and, left to fester, ferments into strikes and coups. See [Citizens & Needs](../civilization/citizens.md).

### S

**Science**
:   The resource your settlement produces as it works and grows, spent in the **Scientific Research** tree on tools, jobs, crafts, walls, and era advancement. Shown as Science per second. See [Research & Eras](../civilization/research-and-eras.md).

**Settlement**
:   Your civilization on the ground: a claimed campfire (the **town hall**), the citizens around it, the territory you hold, and the faction banner that binds it all together. See [Settlements & Growth](../civilization/settlements.md).

**Skill**
:   A worker's mastery of their trade, climbing five tiers by *doing* the job — **Novice → Apprentice → Journeyman → Veteran → Master**. Higher skill lifts the quality ceiling; only a Master reliably makes a craft's finest goods. See [Citizens & Needs](../civilization/citizens.md).

**Stamina**
:   The energy pool that work drains from a citizen. When it runs dry they stop and rest ("Resting — no stamina") rather than push on, then return once it recharges. See [Citizens & Needs](../civilization/citizens.md).

**Stockpile**
:   A block that binds up to eight nearby containers into **one logical store** your stockers, workshops, and trade all treat as a single set of shelves. Flip **Show for trading** on and it becomes a trading pool for diplomacy. See [Workshops & Storage](../civilization/workshops.md).

**Stocker**
:   The hauler job — a worker who carries ingredients *to* workshops and moves finished goods *between* stores, and delivers tools to worker tool storage. Without one, crafters sit idle waiting on materials. See [Jobs & Labor](../civilization/jobs-and-labor.md).

### T

**Tannery**
:   The craft of scraping, curing, and drying raw hides into leather at a **Tanning Rack** (with a clay tank of curing liquid), worked by a **Tanner**. See [Tannery](../antiquity/tannery.md).

**Territory**
:   The set of claimed **chunks** your settlement owns and protects. Everything you build must sit inside it, and claims are limited by a per-era cap. Inside your border, outsiders can't interfere — unless you're at war. See [Territory & Outposts](../civilization/territory.md).

**Tool Age**
:   A civilization-wide ceiling on the *best* tool a worker may equip, climbing **Bone → Wood → Stone → Copper/Tin → Bronze → Iron** as you research. Hand a worker something above your age and you're told it's "too advanced." See [Tool Tiers & Ages](../antiquity/tool-tiers.md).

**Totemic**
:   One of the two paths a [Faith](../civilization/faith.md) can follow: you raise **totem poles** and perform powerful rituals that ask something back from the faithful. Its counterpart is the **Astrology** path. See [Faith & the Stars](../civilization/faith.md).

**Town** *(stage)*
:   A mature, bustling settlement of many households and workshops — the stage above Village and below City. See [Settlements & Growth](../civilization/settlements.md).

**Town Hall**
:   Your claimed campfire — the beating heart of the settlement and the block that opens every menu (research, citizens, labor, policies, votes, diplomacy, and later crisis decisions). Guard it well. See [Settlements & Growth](../civilization/settlements.md).

**Trade**
:   Exchanging goods with another settlement or city-state through the Diplomacy tab. It needs the **Bartering** research, peace, and a **trading stockpile** on both sides — and the goods travel the world on foot by courier, so a deal can be lost on the road. See [Diplomacy, Trade & War](../civilization/diplomacy.md).

**Tribe** *(stage)*
:   The stage a Hearth becomes once you enact a code of laws and choose a government — a people now guided by law. See [Settlements & Growth](../civilization/settlements.md).

### V

**Vassal**
:   A captured settlement or city-state kept as a **subordinate** — bound beneath your banner rather than razed or annexed. See [Diplomacy](../civilization/diplomacy.md).

**Village** *(stage)*
:   The stage your settlement reaches at 25 citizens — the camp has become a real community. See [Settlements & Growth](../civilization/settlements.md).

**Vote**
:   A clickable chat ballot that settles a settlement's weighty choices — exile, war, peace, policies, palettes, disbanding. A **Council** decides most things this way, and even a **Chiefdom** routes its heaviest acts through a vote. See [Government & Law](../civilization/government.md).

### W

**Wall**
:   A fortification you design piece by piece — **Segment**, **Corner**, and **Gate** — in a 3D editor, then have **Builders** raise along your territory border. A wall factors into your safety, population cap, and the raid pressure you draw. See [Walls & Defenses](../civilization/walls.md).

**Woodworking**
:   The craft of sawing logs into planks, sticks, stairs, and frames at a **Woodworking Table**, worked by a **Woodworker**. It unlocks the Wood Age and the fences and Foreman's Rod your settlement leans on. See [Woodworking](../antiquity/woodworking.md).

**Workshop**
:   A production region you mark with the Workshop Orders rod around a **work block** (a Crafting Stone, Masonry bench, and so on). Staffed by a Crafter and supplied by a Stocker, it fulfills the orders you set on its Stock tab. See [Workshops & Storage](../civilization/workshops.md).
