# Your First Hour

<p class="bb-lead">You spawn into an empty land with nothing but your two hands. This is the one stretch of Bannerbound where you really are a lone survivor — knapping stone, cutting fiber, splitting firewood. It ends the moment you light a campfire and found a settlement. From there, you lead. Let's get you there fast.</p>

!!! abstract "The one-hour path, at a glance"

    **Stone Rocks → cobblestone → a blade → a Crafting Stone → firewood → a lit campfire → your settlement.** Every step below feeds the next. You are only hand-crafting to bootstrap; the second you have citizens, they take over the gathering.

## You lead — you don't solo

Before you touch a single rock, hold this thought: **Bannerbound is a civilization game, not a survival grind.** You are the leader of a band of citizens, not a lone adventurer. Everything you do by hand in this first hour exists only to reach a campfire you can found a settlement on. After that, your job is to *organize* — assign [jobs](../civilization/jobs-and-labor.md), direct [research](../civilization/research-and-eras.md), and keep your [citizens](../civilization/citizens.md) fed and happy while *they* mine, chop, and build.

If you ever find yourself mining for an hour, stop and ask which citizen should be doing it instead.

!!! tip "Open the Chronicle first"

    Press ++j++ at any time to open the **Chronicle**, your in-game guidebook. It fills in as you discover systems, and a side tracker walks you through these exact first steps. Don't want the hand-holding? Unpin **Starting Out** in the Chronicle to hide the tracker — you can re-pin it later. See [Codex & Ponder](../reference/codex-and-ponder.md) for more.

## The checklist

Here is the whole first hour in order. Each step is detailed below.

- [ ] Pick up loose **Stone Rocks** and craft 4 into **Cobblestone** (2×2 grid)
- [ ] Hunt an animal for **Bone**, then knap it into **Bone Blades**
- [ ] Knap **Gravel** for **Flint**, then knap Flint into a **Flint Blade**
- [ ] Cut grass and ferns for **Plant Fiber**, and leaves for **Sticks**
- [ ] Combine a Flint Blade + a stick + Plant Fiber into a **Flint Knife** (2×2 grid)
- [ ] Carve a **Crafting Stone** from cobblestone
- [ ] Make an axe, split logs into **Firewood** on a **Chopping Stump**
- [ ] Stack Firewood into a **Campfire**, light it with **Fire Sticks**, and found your settlement

## Step 1 — Cobblestone from Stone Rocks

This first trick is the one the game explains least, so learn it now: **you don't need a pickaxe to get your first cobblestone.**

Look down. Scattered across the ground are small loose **Stone Rocks** — pick them up by hand, no tool required. Gather at least **four**.

Then open your inventory and use the **2×2 crafting grid** in the top-right corner. Place **4 Stone Rocks**, one in each slot, to craft a single block of **Cobblestone**.

!!! warning "Don't go looking for a stone pickaxe yet"

    You can't mine stone the vanilla way this early — there's no wooden pickaxe path. Loose Stone Rocks *are* your stone supply until citizens start quarrying. Grab a small stack of them whenever you see them; you'll want a few blocks of cobblestone before you're done.

You now have your first block of worked stone. Set it aside — it becomes your Crafting Stone in Step 6.

## Step 2 — A first edge: Bone Blades

Your fastest cutting tool comes from animals. Find a pig, cow, sheep, or similar wild animal and hunt it — bare fists work in a pinch, though it's slow. Alongside meat, you'll collect **Bone**.

Now knap it: hold a piece of Bone and **right-click a hard stone surface** — a natural stone block, your fresh cobblestone, or any exposed rock. Each Bone knaps into **2 Bone Blades**.

A **Bone Blade** cuts and gathers all day without ever wearing out — it's only ever *used up* when a recipe consumes it as an ingredient. Knap a few and keep them handy: you'll want **at least three** later to carve a Bone Axe.

!!! note "Blade vs. knife — what carves what"

    A **Bone Blade** cuts plants and gathers fiber and sticks, but it *cannot* carve a Crafting Stone. Only a **Flint Blade** or a **knife** does that. So a Bone Blade is a great head start, but you still want flint. Full detail lives in [Knapping](../antiquity/knapping.md) and [Tool Tiers](../antiquity/tool-tiers.md).

## Step 3 — Flint from gravel, then a Flint Blade

Find or dig up **Gravel** (riverbanks and beaches are full of it). Hold a piece and **right-click a hard stone surface** to smash it. Each smash consumes the gravel and has roughly a **1-in-4 (25%)** chance to flake off a piece of **Flint** — so bring a decent handful of gravel.

Once you have Flint, knap it the same way: **right-click a hard stone surface with Flint** to shape a **Flint Blade**.

The Flint Blade is the keystone tool of your first hour. It cuts plants, and — unlike the Bone Blade — it **carves your first Crafting Stone**.

!!! tip "Knap on the good stuff"

    "Hard stone surface" means natural stone, cobblestone, or exposed rock — not dirt, grass, or sand. If a right-click does nothing, you're aiming at the wrong block. Stand over a patch of stone (or drop your own cobblestone) and knap on that.

## Step 4 — Plant Fiber and Sticks

With any blade in hand, the world becomes raw material:

| You break… | With… | You may get… | Chance |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Grass or ferns | A blade or knife | **Plant Fiber** | ~50% |
| Leaves | A blade or knife | **Sticks** | ~40% |

Sweep through tall grass and ferns to stockpile **Plant Fiber** (your early cordage and thatch), and cut a few leaf blocks for **Sticks**. You only need a couple of sticks right now, but both pile up fast — grab extra while you're here.

## Step 5 — A Flint Knife

A **knife** does the same cutting and carving work as a bare blade, and it doubles as a light weapon for hunting and defense. Here's the part players often get backwards: a **Flint Blade never wears out** from cutting or carving — it does those jobs forever and is only ever used up when a recipe consumes it. So don't make a knife for durability; your Flint Blade alone can carry you through the next step, which is why this one is **recommended, not required**. Make it for the combat edge — and so your knapped blades stay free as crafting stock.

Back to the **2×2 inventory crafting grid**. Combine a **Flint Blade**, a **Stick**, and a piece of **Plant Fiber** to assemble a **Flint Knife** — the fiber is the binding that lashes the edge to the haft.

!!! example "Where you stand right now"

    You've got cobblestone, at least one Flint Blade (and maybe a Flint Knife), a handful of Plant Fiber, and a few Sticks — all from bare hands. That's everything you need to carve your first real workstation.

## Step 6 — Carve your first Crafting Stone

The **Crafting Stone** is your primitive workbench. It's carved, not placed from a recipe.

Place a block of **Cobblestone** in the world, then hold a **Flint Blade** (or any knife) and **right-click the cobblestone**. It transforms in place into a **Crafting Stone**.

!!! note "Sandstone works too"

    A Flint Blade or knife also carves a Crafting Stone from **Sandstone** or **Red Sandstone** — handy if you spawned in a desert with little loose stone around.

### Using the Crafting Stone

The Crafting Stone crafts by **stacking loose ingredients on top**, in any order:

- [ ] Hold an ingredient and **right-click** the stone to place one item onto it.
- [ ] Add each ingredient the recipe needs — order doesn't matter.
- [ ] When the pile matches a recipe, a **floating result preview** appears above the stone.
- [ ] **Shift-right-click** the stone to finish the craft, then pick up the result.

Try it now: add **2 Sticks** to the Crafting Stone, wait for the preview, and shift-right-click to make **Fire Sticks** — your fire-starter for the very last step. Everything about this workstation is covered in [Knapping](../antiquity/knapping.md) and the Crafting Stone entry of your Chronicle.

## Step 7 — Firewood on a Chopping Stump

Now for fuel. First, a gotcha that stops a lot of new players cold:

!!! warning "Logs can't be punched out"

    Bare fists won't knock a log loose from a tree — you **need an axe**. The earliest is a **Bone Axe**, carved at your **Crafting Stone** from **3 Bone Blades, 2 Sticks, and 1 Plant Fiber** (this is why Step 2 told you to keep a few spare blades). No axe means no logs, and no logs means no firewood.

With an axe in hand, fell a tree and gather some logs. Then:

1. **Carve a Chopping Stump.** Place a single, **isolated** log on the ground — not touching other logs — and **right-click it with your axe**. It becomes a **Chopping Stump**. (Connected tree logs are ignored, so make sure the log stands alone.)
2. **Split Firewood.** Put logs onto the stump, then **right-click it with an axe** to chop. Each swing has about an **85%** chance (roughly six of every seven strokes) to produce a piece of **Firewood**. Keep swinging until you have **at least four pieces**.

Firewood is the fuel that replaces charcoal in your earliest recipes — most importantly, the campfire. There's much more to timber in [Woodworking](../antiquity/woodworking.md).

## Step 8 — Build a Campfire and found your settlement

The finish line. The **Campfire** is stacked by hand, no crafting grid needed:

1. **Place 1 Firewood** on the ground.
2. **Add 3 more** Firewood to it. The **fourth piece turns the pile into an unlit Campfire.**
3. **Light it.** Hold your **Fire Sticks**, aim at the unlit Campfire, and **hold the use button for about 2 seconds** until it catches.

!!! success "Now — found your settlement"

    **Right-click the lit Campfire** to open the settlement flow. This is the hinge of the whole game: your lonely hearth becomes a town hall, and you stop being a survivor and start being a leader. Walk through it in [Founding a Settlement](founding-a-settlement.md).

!!! tip "Site the campfire with intent"

    Where you light this fire is where your settlement takes root, so glance around first. You want flat, defensible ground near wood, stone, and water — but don't agonize. You can expand [territory](../civilization/territory.md) later. Getting *a* settlement founded matters more than getting the perfect spot.

## What you skipped (and can come back for)

You reached a campfire on the fastest possible path. A few side tools are worth knowing about for the hours ahead:

Foreman's Rod
: The tool you'll reach for constantly once you have citizens — it's how you assign jobs and mark out work sites. Carve one at your **Crafting Stone** from **1 Stick + 1 Plant Fiber**. See [Jobs & Labor](../civilization/jobs-and-labor.md).

Stone Spear
: Knapped for the hunt — a thrown or thrusting weapon that brings back meat, hide, and feather. See [Fletching & Archery](../antiquity/fletching-and-archery.md).

Primitive Bow
: A ranged option for hunting skittish animals from a distance.

Bone & Wooden Knives
: Knives that double as light weapons — a step up from a bare blade for hunting and defense. Craft a **Bone Knife** once you have a Crafting Stone, and later a **Wooden Knife** at a woodworking table.

None of these block your first settlement — but they make feeding and defending it far easier.
