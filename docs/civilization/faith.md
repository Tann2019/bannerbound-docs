# Faith & the Stars

<p class="bb-lead"><em>Sooner or later your people stop looking at the ground and start looking up. When they do, you can give their wonder a shape — a Faith of your own making, with gods you literally draw into the stars.</em></p>

!!! abstract "In short"
    - **Unlock** faith by completing the **Spiritualism** [culture doctrine](culture-and-heraldry.md); the people then gather at the **town hall** to choose one.
    - **Found** a brand-new faith (name it, pick a path) or **adopt** an existing one. The leader opens the choice, the people vote, and a clear majority carries it — a divided vote is thrown out and begins again.
    - Two paths: **Astrology** (quiet blessings from drawn constellations) and **Totemic** (powerful totem-pole rituals that ask something back).
    - Faith runs on **Devotion**, a resource that trickles in from every believer and pools across the whole faith.
    - Astrology's heart is **Pantheon Mode**: stand under a clear night sky, connect **3–12 stars** into a constellation, name it and its god, and spend Devotion to raise it. Its **domain** — and the blessing it grants — comes from the colors of its stars.

## The people look up

A faith sits *above* your [settlement](settlements.md). It is the first thing in your world bigger than any single banner: a body that can spread from town to town and bind many settlements together under one sky. Choosing one is as weighty as adopting a code of laws.

You cannot found a faith on day one. It opens only once your people are ready to believe — when they "look to the heavens and the old stones." In practice that means completing **Spiritualism**, an early doctrine on the [culture](culture-and-heraldry.md) tree. Finish it and a message goes out:

!!! quote ""
    *The people look to the heavens and the old stones. A Faith may be founded — gather at the town hall.*

From then on, your town hall carries a new **Faith** menu.

## Founding or adopting a faith

Open the **Faith** menu at the town hall and you'll see **Choose a Faith** — *"Found a new faith, or adopt one. Majority wins."* The faith is the **leader's** to open, but the people decide.

You have two options:

=== "Found a new faith"

    Give it a **name** of your own and pick one of the two **paths**. This is your faith — its gods, its sky, its story all start blank and become whatever you make them. A faith **must** be named before it can be founded.

=== "Adopt an existing faith"

    If a faith already exists elsewhere in the world, you can embrace it instead of starting fresh. Your settlement joins the ranks of the faithful, shares its Devotion into the same pool, and inherits its gods and research. This is how a faith **spreads** across the map.

However the people choose, a **clear majority** carries the vote. If the people are split, nothing is chosen:

!!! warning "A divided people believe in nothing"
    *"The people are divided — no faith was chosen. The vote begins anew."* If your citizens can't agree, the choice simply restarts. Rally your people behind one option before you put it to them.

Changing your mind later is possible but deliberate. A settlement can **abandon** its faith, but doing so takes its own vote — a set number of citizens must back it, tracked as *"N of M needed have voted to abandon the faith."* Abandoning **forfeits every point of stored devotion**, and afterward there's a cooldown before the people are ready again — *"The people are not yet ready to believe again."* Faith is not something you flip on and off.

### The two paths

Both paths speak the same language of blessings; they just express it differently.

| Path | How the gods reach you | Feel |
|------|------------------------|------|
| **Astrology** | You draw constellations in the night sky. Each god grants a **quiet, passive** benefit for as long as it burns above you. | Peaceful, permanent, hands-off. |
| **Totemic** | You raise **totem poles** and perform rituals before them. The gods are *here*, and their power is greater — but they **make demands** of the faithful. | Active, potent, higher-stakes. |

!!! info "This page focuses on Astrology"
    Astrology's signature feature — literally drawing your gods into the stars — is unique to Bannerbound and is covered in full below. The Totemic path begins with the **Ritual Roots** faith research, which teaches your people how to carve and raise the first totem pole.

## Devotion

Every faith runs on one resource: **Devotion**. You'll see it tracked at the town hall as a stored total and a rate, written **`Devotion: N (+N/s)`**.

Devotion is generated by belief itself, and it grows along a few familiar lines:

- **More believers.** Every citizen of a faithful settlement adds a small, steady trickle. Grow your [citizens](citizens.md) and the offerings grow with them.
- **More devoted players.** Each player who belongs to a faithful settlement lifts its rate too — two players sharing a town out-pray a lone founder.
- **More settlements in the faith.** Devotion **pools across the whole faith** — every member town's rate flows into one shared pool. A faith that has spread across the map accrues far faster than a lone settlement.
- **Kinship gods.** Some constellations (see below) grant **+devotion/s** directly, letting your faith help fund itself.

Devotion is what you **spend** to advance. It fuels your faith research, and it's the price of raising each new god into the sky.

## Faith research

Your faith has its own progression, separate from science and culture. Open the **Faith** tab in the research screen — its currency isn't research points but **devotion**, and it advances on its own, **in parallel** with your normal [research](research-and-eras.md). You don't have to choose between studying the world and studying the heavens.

Set a node active and your faith's pooled devotion pours into it until it's complete. Only your chosen path's branch is visible and researchable — pick Astrology and the Totemic nodes stay hidden, and vice versa.

| Faith research | Path | Devotion cost | What it gives |
|----------------|------|:---:|---------------|
| **Communal Rites** | Shared root | 10 | The first shared observance; a small **culture/s** boost. |
| **Devout Hearts** | Shared | 20 | Believing hands waste nothing — a small **food/s** boost. |
| **The Heavens' Wheel** | Shared | 36 | Watching the turning sky sharpens the mind — a small **science/s** boost. |
| **Star Charts** | :material-star: Astrology | 24 | Names the colored stars so you can **read a god's domain** — and opens **Pantheon Mode**. |
| **Greater Pantheon** | :material-star: Astrology | 40 | Raises a **second seat** in the heavens (one more god slot). |
| **Exalted Pantheon** | :material-star: Astrology | 70 | A **third** watcher over the banner (another god slot). |
| **Ritual Roots** | :material-campfire: Totemic | 24 | The foundation of the **totem pole** and the rites performed before it. |

!!! tip "Chart the stars before you bind them"
    **Star Charts** is the gateway to everything below. Until you research it, the night sky's colored stars keep their secrets — a constellation you draw will show *"Domain: ? (research Star Charts)"* — and you can't enter Pantheon Mode at all. Make it an early priority on the Astrology path.

## The Pantheon

Your **Pantheon** is the roster of gods you've drawn — the constellations that watch over your people. It has a limited number of **slots**: you begin with room for a **single god** and raise more seats with the **Greater Pantheon** and **Exalted Pantheon** research above — three gods in all once both are done. Each god fills one slot, burns as a constellation in the real night sky, and contributes its blessing to your settlement every second.

### Entering Pantheon Mode

There's no menu-driven star map here — **the sky itself is the screen.** To draw a god, open your Faith at the town hall and choose **Enter Pantheon Mode**. Before it will open, all of the following must be true:

- [ ] Your faith follows the **Astrology** path.
- [ ] You've researched **Star Charts**.
- [ ] It's **night** and the weather is **clear** — if the heavens are washed out you'll be told *"The heavens are veiled."*
- [ ] You're standing under **open sky**, with nothing overhead — otherwise, *"You must stand under the open sky."*

When it opens: *"The heavens open. Connect the stars, and name what you find."*

### Drawing a constellation

In Pantheon Mode your crosshair hovers over the nearest star. Trace a line from star to star to shape your god:

| Control | Action |
|---------|--------|
| **Left-click** | Connect the star you're aiming at to your line. |
| **Right-click** or **R** | Undo the last connection. |
| **Enter** | Name your constellation (available once you have at least 3 stars). |
| **Esc** | Leave Pantheon Mode. |

A constellation must be **at least 3 and at most 12 stars**. A tidy heads-up display tracks your progress and shows the control legend the whole time.

!!! tip "Branch and trim"
    Click a star that's **already** part of your chain and the line trims back to it — a quick way to undo a whole tail or start a new branch without unwinding star by star.

### Naming your god

Press **Enter** and you'll name two things: the **constellation** itself and the **deity** who lives in it. Both are required — *"The constellation and its god must both be named."* Names must be unique within your faith's sky; you can't write the same name twice among the stars.

This is also where you'll see the god's **domain**, and where you commit the **devotion** to raise it. Confirm, and the sky answers:

!!! quote ""
    *[your god] ascends. The constellation [its name] burns in the night sky.*

## Reading the stars

A god's power comes entirely from the **colors of the stars** you chose. Once **Star Charts** is researched, each colored ("typed") star reveals which **domain** it belongs to. There are seven:

| Star | Domain | The god's blessing |
|------|--------|--------------------|
| :material-star: Gold | **Harvest** | more **food/s** |
| :material-star: Red | **War** | quicker citizens (**move speed**) |
| :material-star: Paired pale | **Kinship** | more **devotion/s** |
| :material-star: Blue | **Knowledge** | more **science/s** |
| :material-star: Amber | **Craft** | more **culture/s** |
| :material-star: Lone pale | **Journey** | quicker citizens (**move speed**) |
| :material-star: Sea-green | **Sea** | more **food/s** |

Two of these hide in plain sight and are worth hunting for:

- **Journey** stars are **lone, pale** points set far from any cluster — off on their own in the dark.
- **Sea** stars glow **sea-green** and hang **low near the horizon**, close to where the sky turns.

Plain, uncolored stars have no domain of their own — they're free to use as connective points, but *"a god needs at least one colored star"* to have any domain at all.

### Pure and hybrid gods

The mix of colors in your constellation decides its domain profile:

- **Pure** — every colored star is the *same* type. The god belongs wholly to one domain, and its blessing is **stronger** — roughly a quarter again as much — for that single-mindedness. The naming screen reads *"Domain: Harvest (pure)"*.
- **Hybrid** — two colored types are present (the second needs at least two stars to count). The god takes the **dominant** color as its primary domain and the runner-up as its secondary, reading *"Domain: Harvest + Sea"*. It grants its primary blessing at normal strength, **plus a bonus** when the two domains have a natural synergy.

!!! example "Two ways to bless the harvest"
    Want more food? A **pure Harvest** god — a constellation of only gold stars — pours out the strongest single food blessing. But a **Harvest + Sea** hybrid, mixing gold with a sea-green star or two, layers a food synergy on top of the base. Pure gods are simple and potent; well-matched hybrids reward players who learn which domains love each other.

### Raising and forgetting gods

Raising a god isn't free — the sky asks for **devotion**, and the price climbs as your pantheon grows. Your **first** god costs **25 devotion**; each one after costs more — the second twice that, the third three times over. If your pool is too shallow you'll be told exactly how much is needed: *"Not enough devotion — N is needed to raise this god."* Let your believers pray a while, then try again.

When every slot is full, you'll see *"The pantheon is full — expand it through faith research."* Research **Greater Pantheon** or **Exalted Pantheon** for another seat.

To make room another way, **forget** a god from the Pantheon list. Its stars are released back to the sky and its light goes out — but the devotion you spent raising it is **gone for good**; the gods give no refunds.

!!! quote ""
    *[your god] is forgotten. The constellation [its name] fades from the sky.*

!!! tip "Turn on your shaders"
    The faith sky is meant to be *seen*. It looks its absolute best with **Iris + Sodium** installed — the colored stars and your glowing constellations really come alive. See [Optional Mods](../reference/optional-mods.md) to set them up.
