# Settlements & Growth

<p class="bb-lead"><em>Every civilization starts as a single spark of firelight. From that claimed campfire — a handful of settlers foraging in the dark — you will grow a Hearth into a Tribe, a Tribe into a Village, and, in time, a Village into a City that fills the horizon.</em></p>

!!! abstract "The short of it"
    A **settlement** is a claimed campfire (your **town hall**), the citizens who gather around it, the territory you claim, and the **faction banner** that binds it all together. It grows in two ways: **immigration** draws in newcomers up to your era's **immigration floor**, and after that **births** carry you higher — but only while you keep a **food surplus** and build enough **beds**. Growth passes named milestones: **Hearth → Tribe → Village → Town → City**.

## What a settlement is

A settlement begins with a **campfire**. Place one in unclaimed land — it sits *unlit* — then right-click it to open the **Found a Settlement** screen, where you name your people and choose your banner color. Confirm, and the now-lit campfire becomes your **town hall**: the beating heart of everything you build. (For the full founding walkthrough, see [Founding a Settlement](../getting-started/founding-a-settlement.md).)

Right-click the town hall and it opens the settlement menus: research, citizens, territory, labor, policies, votes, and later your crisis decisions. Wherever this page says "the town hall," picture that first campfire (or the block you later promote to hold the role).

Four things make a settlement whole:

- **A town hall** — the claimed campfire that anchors your people and opens every menu.
- **Citizens** — the folk who live, work, and grow your civilization. See [Citizens](citizens.md).
- **Claimed territory** — the chunks you own and protect. See [Territory](territory.md).
- **A faction banner** — the standard your settlement is *bound* to. See [Culture & Heraldry](culture-and-heraldry.md).

!!! warning "Keep your banner flying"
    Your settlement is bound to its **faction banner** — a block distinct from the campfire. Raise the official banner within **24 blocks** of the town hall, with a clear access side to reach it, and your people are under command. If that standard is taken down or falls, the settlement is *unbound* — **no menus, no work** — until you raise it again. Treat the banner like a heartbeat, not decoration.

New members join through **registration tablets** and **registration papers**: documents you issue at the town hall that let another player enlist in your settlement. Each [era](research-and-eras.md) you advance raises how many registration documents you can hand out.

## The growth ladder

As your population climbs, your settlement crosses named thresholds. Each **stage-up** is a moment worth celebrating — the whole settlement hears the news in chat, and a volley of fireworks bursts above the town hall.

| Stage | What it marks | The herald in chat |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| :material-campfire: **Hearth** | Your founding camp, before any government. A tiny band of settlers around the fire. | *(the beginning)* |
| :material-account-group: **Tribe** | You have enacted a government — a Code of Laws now guides your people. | *"[Name] has become a Tribe."* |
| :material-home-group: **Village** | Your population reaches **25 citizens** — the camp has become a real community. | *"[Name] has grown into a Village!"* |
| :material-town-hall: **Town** | A mature, bustling settlement of many households and workshops. | *"[Name] has risen to a Town!"* |
| :material-city: **City** | The grand milestone — a sprawling seat of civilization. | *"[Name] is now a City!"* |

!!! note "Hearth to Tribe is a choice, not just a headcount"
    You leave the **Hearth** stage when you enact a government, not simply by growing. Immigration first fills your camp up to the immigration floor; once you're there, the town hall announces *"It is time to enact the code of laws"* and invites you to choose how you'll be ruled. Making that choice — and hearing *"[Name] has become a Tribe"* light up the sky — turns your Hearth into a **Tribe**. See [Government](government.md) for the forms you can pick.

## How your population grows

Two very different forces feed your growth, and it helps to know which one you're leaning on.

### Immigration: newcomers on the road

In the early game your numbers swell through **immigration** — settlers who hear of your fire and come to join it. Two things drive it:

- **Culture is the currency.** Your settlement steadily builds culture, and the **progress-to-next-citizen** bar you see at the town hall is that culture filling up. When it's full, a newcomer arrives and the cost is spent. Newcomers come one at a time, with a pause between each, so growth feels like a steady trickle rather than a flood. Culture comes largely from your claimed land and researches — see [Culture & Heraldry](culture-and-heraldry.md).
- **Food is the gate.** Food is **not** spent per citizen — it's a green light. A settlement only takes in newcomers while it's *feeding itself*: while your **net food** is at or above zero. Slip into a deficit and immigration pauses until your fields, herds, and hunters catch up.

Open the town hall and read the **Food** bar. It tells you plainly where you stand:

=== "Surplus"

    The bar reads **Surplus**, and the growth gate confirms: *"Surplus — the settlement can take in newcomers."* Your production covers your people with room to spare, so your buffer tops up and growth is on. This is where you want to live.

=== "Draining"

    The bar reads **Draining** — you're producing less than you eat, so your stored food is trickling away (the town hall shows roughly how many days are left). Because net food has gone negative, **growth pauses**: *"growth pauses until food production recovers."* Add food workers or fields *before* the buffer empties.

=== "Starving!"

    The bar reads **Starving!** Your buffer has run dry and your citizens begin to **starve**. Growth stays paused and lives are on the line. Fix food *before* people, always.

!!! tip "Grow food and housing *ahead* of your people, not behind them"
    Every new citizen is another pair of hands — and another mouth. The golden rule of a healthy settlement is to build your fields and your beds *before* the population that needs them. A settlement that outruns its food starves; one that outruns its beds can't have children. Read [Food](food.md) and [Housing](housing.md) alongside this page.

### The immigration floor

Immigration doesn't run forever. Each era sets an **immigration floor** — the population that newcomers will top you up to. In the founding **Ancient** era that floor is **seven citizens**. Immigration keeps bringing settlers while your population is *below* the floor, and stops once you reach it.

Crucially, the floor is a *floor*, not a lifetime cap. If disaster strikes and your citizens die off, your population drops back below the floor and **immigration resumes automatically** — you can never be permanently locked out of rebuilding. Later eras raise the floor higher, so advancing your [research and era](research-and-eras.md) lifts the ceiling on free immigration.

!!! warning "Below the floor, you can't choose a government"
    Selecting a government needs your population at or above the immigration floor. If your numbers fall under it mid-decision, you'll see *"Government selection paused — population dropped below the immigration floor,"* and the choice waits until your people recover. Keep yourself comfortably above the floor before you reach for the Code of Laws.

### Births: growing past the floor

Once you've hit the immigration floor, the road onward is **births**. Two adult citizens of **opposite gender** who **share a home**, sleep under the same roof, and have grown to *like* one another can conceive during the night — you'll see hearts rise above them, and a little one arrives before long. Children grow into working adults in time.

But births have their own gate: your **population maximum** (shown at the town hall as *Population: X / Y*). That maximum sits at your immigration floor **until you build spare beds** — every bed beyond your current people raises the cap and gives a couple room for a child.

!!! warning "House everyone before adding spare beds"
    Spare beds only count toward your population maximum when **nobody is homeless**. If even a single citizen has no home, the cap stays put no matter how many extra beds you lay — the game won't pretend to support new families while it still can't house the ones you have. Roof everyone first, *then* add the beds that let your people grow.

In short:

- [ ] **Below the floor?** → newcomers immigrate to fill your ranks.
- [ ] **At the floor?** → house everyone, add **spare beds** so families can grow, and keep the **food surplus** flowing.
- [ ] **Want a Village?** → sustain all three until you cross **25 citizens**.

!!! example "From spark to Tribe"
    You place a campfire in a quiet valley and found the Hearth of *Emberfall*. With no government yet, your camp eats nothing at all, and your founding settlers **forage** — a small food trickle labelled *Pioneering spirit* in the town hall. That surplus lets culture build, and one by one, settlers arrive: *"…has joined the settlement of Emberfall."* When Emberfall reaches seven souls, the town hall announces *"It is time to enact the code of laws."* You gather your people, choose a government — and the sky lights up: **"Emberfall has become a Tribe."** From here you raise houses with spare beds, and the first children of Emberfall are born.

## Pioneering spirit

At founding you have almost no one, and — because a Hearth has no government yet — **nothing is being eaten at all**. So where does the early food surplus come from? Your **founding settlers forage** to grow the camp. Open the town hall's Food readout in these first days and you'll see the line **"Pioneering spirit: +[x]/s — Founding settlers forage to grow the camp."**

That foraging trickle is what carries a brand-new Hearth off the ground: it keeps you in surplus so culture can build and your first immigrants can arrive, long before you've plowed a field or fenced a pen.

!!! info "Consumption switches on with your government"
    While you're still a Hearth, your citizens draw nothing from the larder — a deliberate grace period so a young camp can build its food economy in peace. The moment you enact the Code of Laws and become a **Tribe**, appetite switches on for good. Have real [food production](food.md) — fields, herds, fishing, hunting — ready to take over before you make that choice.

## Ending a settlement

Not every settlement lasts. A people can be disbanded by choice — or collapse against their will.

### Disbanding — the peaceful end

To end a settlement deliberately, call a **disband vote** at the town center (the town hall). Anyone can start it, and the whole settlement is told: *"…started a vote on disbanding the colony! Vote at the town center. Expires in 3 minutes."*

- The window lasts **3 minutes**.
- The vote must be **unanimous** — *every* member has to agree. When they do: *"Disband vote passed unanimously. Settlement disbanding…"*
- If even one member doesn't vote before the timer runs out, the vote expires and nothing happens.

!!! danger "Disbanding is permanent — and blocked under attack"
    Disbanding **permanently removes your citizens and claims** — there is no undo. And you **cannot disband while your settlement is under attack**: *"You can't disband while your settlement is under attack — survive the raid or make peace first."* Weather the [raid](barbarians.md) or make [peace](diplomacy.md) before you can lay the settlement to rest.

### Collapse — the unwilling end

A settlement can also end without a vote. If its **last member leaves**, there's no one left to hold it together, and it **collapses** — the whole server hears *"The settlement of [Name] has collapsed."* Its claims are released and its ground becomes open country once more.

Losing your **town hall** is its own catastrophe. The campfire town hall is protected like the rest of your territory — outsiders can't break it — but if it *is* destroyed, your people are told *"The town hall of [Name] has been destroyed,"* and your settlement is left without its center until you re-establish one. Guard it well.
