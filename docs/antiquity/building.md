# Building Materials

<p class="bb-lead"><em>A settlement is only as grand as what it's built from. Long before quarried stone and metal, you roof your homes in golden thatch, mould mud into brick, burn limestone into lime, and dress bare walls with painted plaster. This is the palette of the early ages — every block here you can make with your own hands.</em></p>

!!! abstract "TL;DR"
    - **Thatch** — dry **Plant Fiber** on a drying rack into a **Thatch Bundle**, then build thatch blocks, slabs, stairs, a door, and an early **Thatch Bed**.
    - **Mud Brick** — spin clay into **Raw Mud Bricks**, build the soft **Unfired Mud Brick** family for a quick shelter, or **fire** raw bricks into hard **Mud Bricks** in a Kiln. Two Mud Bricks + a Block of Coal make a [Bloomery](metalworking.md).
    - **Limestone** — burn **Limestone** into **Quicklime** in a kiln, or cut it into **Limestone Bricks** (bricks, stairs, slab, wall).
    - **Plaster & Paint** — coat any face with **Plaster** for appeal and toughness, then stamp colored **trims** with the **Paint Brush**.
    - **Fences** — tie **Rope Fences** and gates together with **Fiber Rope**, or place simple **Stick Fences**, in every wood type.
    - **Rafts** — right-click a straight line of **3 Thatch blocks** with an **Oar** to lash a raft you can sail, leash, and hitch to a fence.

Everything on this page is Antiquity-era: no crafting table required. You'll shape most of it on a [Crafting Stone](knapping.md) or a [Pottery Slab](pottery.md), fire it in a Kiln, or simply lash it together in the world. Keep these materials flowing and your [builders](../civilization/housing.md) will raise real homes from them.

## Thatch — the first roof

**Thatch** is dried grass bundled into a golden building block, and it's the earliest way to roof and wall a home. It starts as the **Plant Fiber** you cut from grass and ferns (see [Knapping & First Tools](knapping.md)).

The trick is drying it. Lay **Plant Fiber** on a **drying rack** and leave it in the open air — after about **30 seconds** it cures into a **Thatch Bundle**, the raw ingredient for everything thatched.

!!! note "Unlocked with Housing"
    Thatch building blocks — the slab, stairs, door, bed, and the bundle itself — become craftable once you complete the **Housing** research. Until then, dried bundles are just a material. See [Housing](../civilization/housing.md).

From bundles, shape the thatch family on a **Crafting Stone** — jump to [these recipes on the Crafting Stone](../reference/recipes.md#crafting-stone) for the whole set, or read them off below:

| You place on the Crafting Stone… | You get | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **4× Thatch Bundle** | **1 Thatch** block | The full block — walls, roofs, floors |
| **1× Thatch** | **2 Thatch Slabs** | Half-height cover |
| **6× Thatch** | **4 Thatch Stairs** | Sloped roofs and eaves |
| **6× Thatch** | **3 Thatch Doors** | A woven doorway |
| **3× Thatch + 3× Thatch Bundle** | **1 Thatch Bed** | A straw mat you can sleep in |

The **Thatch Bed** behaves like any bed — sleep through the night and set your respawn point — but it lies low as a flat straw mat rather than a raised frame. It's the cheapest bed you can make, perfect for a settlement's first cramped homes before you can afford wool.

!!! tip "Thatch is also a raft"
    A straight line of three Thatch blocks isn't just a floor — right-click it with an **Oar** and it becomes a boat. See [Rafts](#rafts) below.

## Mud Brick — earth into stone

Where clay is plentiful, **Mud Brick** is your cheapest sturdy building material. It walks through three stages — wet, sun-dried, and kiln-fired — and the whole line (raw bricks, the soft block family, and the hard fired brick) opens up with the [Pottery](pottery.md) research.

**1. Shape the raw bricks.** On a **Pottery Slab**, spin a lump of **Clay** into **Raw Mud Bricks** — each clay block gives you a handful. This is basic [pottery](pottery.md) work.

**2. Build the unfired family.** On a **Crafting Stone**, press raw bricks into the **Unfired Mud Brick** blocks and their variants:

| You place on the Crafting Stone… | You get |
| --- | --- |
| **4× Raw Mud Brick** | **4 Unfired Mud Bricks** |
| **3× Unfired Mud Bricks** | **6 Unfired Mud Brick Slabs** |
| **6× Unfired Mud Bricks** | **4 Unfired Mud Brick Stairs** |
| **6× Unfired Mud Bricks** | **6 Unfired Mud Brick Walls** |

Unfired mud brick is soft, sun-dried earth — fine for a quick shelter, but crumbly. The unfired blocks stay soft; when you want proper, durable brick, you fire the *raw bricks* instead.

**3. Fire it hard.** Drop **Raw Mud Bricks** into a **Kiln**. Firing drives out the moisture and bakes soft earth into tough, weatherproof **Mud Bricks** — the same warm ochre brick you see in early settlements. This is the block your smelter is built from.

!!! warning "Sun-dried brick is fragile"
    Don't build a wall you mean to keep out of the *unfired* blocks — they're a placeholder. Fire **Raw Mud Bricks** into hard Mud Bricks first, or a barbarian raid will find the soft spots. See [Barbarians](../civilization/barbarians.md).

### Mud brick builds the Bloomery

Fired **Mud Bricks** do more than wall a house — they're how you build your first smelter. Stack **two Mud Bricks** where you want a furnace, then right-click the lower block with a **Block of Coal**, and the pair forms a **Bloomery**. That's your gateway to smelting ore and casting metal — the whole story is in [Metalworking](metalworking.md).

## Limestone — brick and lime

**Limestone** is a pale stone that generates in the world and does double duty in your build stock. You can cut it into handsome bricks *or* burn it into quicklime for the tannery. Limestone and its brick set come into reach once you complete the [Masonry](masonry.md) research.

### Quicklime

Bake **Limestone** in a **Kiln** and it calcines into **Quicklime** — a caustic white powder. Quicklime's main job is at the [Tannery](tannery.md): dissolved in a clay tank of water, it becomes the curing liquid that turns a scraped hide into leather. Keep a kiln burning limestone whenever your tanner's quicklime stock runs low.

### Limestone Bricks

For building, dress limestone on a **Crafting Stone** into a full brick set:

| You place on the Crafting Stone… | You get |
| --- | --- |
| **4× Limestone** | **4 Limestone Bricks** |
| **3× Limestone Bricks** | **6 Limestone Brick Slabs** |
| **6× Limestone Bricks** | **4 Limestone Brick Stairs** |
| **6× Limestone Bricks** | **6 Limestone Brick Walls** |

Limestone Bricks are a cleaner, cooler-toned counterpart to mud brick — a fine facing for civic buildings and walls. For dressing raw stone into slabs, stairs, and polished blocks *in bulk*, pair this with the [Masonry](masonry.md) bench.

## Plaster & decoration

A bare wall is functional; a *dressed* wall is a home. **Plaster** and painted **trim** aren't blocks — they're thin decoration layers you apply to the face of a block that's already standing. Because they sit flush against the surface, the space next to the wall stays free for torches, storage, or a doorway.

Both the plaster and the **Paint Brush** come with the [Pottery](pottery.md) research, alongside the mud-brick line.

### Plaster

Make **Plaster** on a **Crafting Stone** from **Clay Balls** and a **Thatch Bundle** — a straw-reinforced daub. Then, holding the plaster item:

- **Right-click a block face** to coat it in plaster.
- **Sneak + right-click** a coated face to strip the plaster back off.

Plaster earns its keep twice over. It raises a home's or workshop's **appeal**, which makes your [citizens](../civilization/citizens.md) more comfortable where they live and work — and it also *hardens* the wall behind it, toughening the block against blasts and breakage.

!!! tip "Wattle and daub"
    For a classic early-village look, frame a wall in [Stick Fences](#fences) or rough timber and then plaster over it — the reeds-and-mud finish reads as genuine wattle-and-daub. It's cheap, it's fast, and it lifts appeal.

### The Paint Brush

The **Paint Brush** stamps a colored decorative shape on top of a face. Hold a **dye in your off hand** to set the color, then right-click the face to stamp the currently selected trim. **Sneak + right-click** cycles through the shapes — including **None**, which wipes the trim off.

You have nine shapes across three families, filled or as an outline:

| Family | Shapes |
| --- | --- |
| **Square** | Square (outline) · Filled Square |
| **Triangle** | Triangle (up) · Filled Triangle · Triangle (down) · Filled Triangle (down) |
| **Wave** | Wave (Top) · Wave (Middle) · Wave (Bottom) |

The three wave shapes are meant to stack vertically — a **Top**, one or more **Middle**, and a **Bottom** run together into one continuous painted wave up a wall. Mix dyes and shapes to give each faction or district its own visual signature; like plaster, trim adds to a building's appeal.

!!! example "Dressing a townhouse"
    1. Coat the front wall in **Plaster** (right-click each face).
    2. Put a **dye** in your off hand — say, terracotta red.
    3. Select **Filled Triangle** on the Paint Brush (sneak-cycle to it) and stamp a gable pattern under the eaves.
    4. Switch to the **Wave** shapes and run a painted band along the base course.

## Fences

Antiquity has two fence lines, both available in **every wood type** — oak, spruce, birch, jungle, acacia, dark oak, mangrove, and cherry. Both open up with the [Woodworking](woodworking.md) research, back in the age of bone tools.

=== "Rope Fences"

    A **Rope Fence** is a bare upright post; a rope slung between two of them is what actually pens the gap. You craft the posts and gates from a log and Fiber Rope — see [the rope fences in the crafting grid](../reference/recipes.md#inventory-crafting-grid). Place your Rope Fences (and **Rope Fence Gates** where you want a way through), then tie the posts together:

    - Hold **Fiber Rope** and **right-click one post**, then **right-click a second post or a gate upright**. A rope stretches between the two tie points.
    - A gate has **two** tie points (its left and right uprights), so a fence line can pass straight through it.
    - **Right-click a gate** with an empty hand to lift or drop its bar — an open or closed gate.

    !!! note "One rope only reaches so far"
        If you try to tie two posts that are too far apart you'll be told the post is *too far away for one rope* — set your posts closer, or add a post in between. Right-clicking again before finishing a tie cancels it.

=== "Stick Fences"

    A **Stick Fence** is the plain, rustic fence — just uprights and rails, no rope, placed and connected like an ordinary fence. It's the quickest way to pen animals or edge a path when you don't need a gate or the rope look.

!!! tip "Fences are more than boundaries"
    Rope Fence Posts also serve as **hitching points**: leash a [raft](#rafts) to yourself, then right-click a fence with **Fiber Rope** to tie the raft up at the water's edge.

## Rafts

A **Raft** is your first watercraft, and you don't craft it — you *lash it together on the water*. Lay a **straight line of three Thatch blocks** floating or on the shore, then **right-click that line with an Oar**. The three blocks fold up into a raft you can climb aboard. The **Oar** itself comes with the [Sailing](../civilization/research-and-eras.md) research, so rafts arrive once you've pushed past the shallows.

- **Sail it** — mount up and steer with your movement keys, just like a boat, paddle sounds and all.
- **Repair it** — a knocked-about raft floats lower in the water as it wears. Right-click it with a **Thatch Bundle** to patch it back up.
- **Hitch it** — a raft can be leashed. Leash it to yourself, then **right-click a fence with Fiber Rope** to tie it off at a dock or bank so it doesn't drift away.

!!! warning "Thatch is thatch"
    A raft is only bundled straw — it takes damage and can be wrecked. Keep a **Thatch Bundle** on hand for long trips, and don't sail your only raft into a barbarian's spear.

## Finishing touches

Two small blocks round out the palette — pure dressing that make a settlement feel lived-in.

**Firewood Pile.** Set a piece of **Firewood** on the ground and keep adding more to grow the stack. It's a decorative woodpile against a wall or beside a hearth — but mind the fourth piece: adding it turns the pile into an unlit **Campfire** (which, on unclaimed land, founds a whole new settlement). See [Founding a Settlement](../getting-started/founding-a-settlement.md).

**Decorative Rocks.** Scattered **Stone Rocks**, **Sandstone Rocks**, and **Red Sandstone Rocks** are loose stones you can place for detail — a rocky garden border, a shoreline, a ruin. They have no collision, so you walk right through them, and they break instantly; drop one into snow and it settles in neatly rather than punching a hole. Each kind also crafts four-to-one into its matching stone block — **four Stone Rocks** give you your very first cobblestone, the knapping trick that bootstraps your first [Crafting Stone](knapping.md); four Sandstone or Red Sandstone Rocks make the matching sandstone.

!!! success "Your Antiquity build kit"
    Thatch for roofs, mud brick and limestone for walls, plaster and paint for appeal, fences and rafts to shape the land and water around them. Stock these in your builders' reach and the settlement stops being a camp and becomes a place people come home to.
