# Pottery & the Kiln

<p class="bb-lead"><em>River clay is soft, cheap, and everywhere. Learn to pack it, shape it, and bake it, and you turn mud into your settlement's first real storage, its first liquid-carriers, and the vessel that will one day carry molten metal.</em></p>

!!! abstract "The short version"
    - Research **Kilns**, then right-click a placed **Cobblestone** with a **Clay Ball** to make **Clayed Cobblestone**. Stack **eight** into a 2×2×2 cube and it collapses into a **Kiln**, mouth facing you.
    - **Fire it:** right-click the mouth to load raw goods, light it with **Flint & Steel**, feed **Coal or Charcoal** to stoke it, then right-click empty-handed once the fire dies to collect the baked results.
    - Research **Pottery**, then right-click a Clayed Cobblestone with **2 Clay Balls** to pack a **Pottery Slab**. Place a block of **Clay** on it, choose a vessel, and shape it with the spin minigame — **hold left-click and spin the mouse**.
    - Shaped pieces come out **unfired**. Harden them in the Kiln into pots, buckets, crucibles, molds, and bricks.
    - The Kiln also bakes **Limestone → Quicklime** — the lime you need for tanning and plaster.

Pottery is the humble backbone of the Bronze Age. Before you can smelt an ingot you need a [crucible](metalworking.md) to hold the melt and a mold to cast it in — and both are ceramics you throw and fire here. This page covers the two stations at the heart of it: the **Kiln** that holds heat, and the **Pottery Slab** where clay takes shape.

---

## Clay, in two forms

It pays to keep two ingredients straight, because the game does:

- **Clay Ball** — the loose lump you get from breaking a clay block. Used to **clay a cobblestone** and to **pack a pottery slab**.
- **Clay** (the block) — a full block, worth four balls. This is what you place on the Pottery Slab to **shape a vessel**.

You'll gather clay along riverbeds, lake shallows, and swamp floors. Bring back plenty — the whole ceramic chain runs on it.

!!! tip "Clay sparks the idea"
    Digging up clay does more than fill your pack — turning a few blocks over sparks **insight** toward the **Pottery** research on the [tech tree](../civilization/research-and-eras.md). Mine while you gather and you'll be closer to the discovery by the time you get home.

## Building the Kiln

The Kiln is the first true furnace of the Antiquity era: a squat dome of clay-packed stone that holds real heat long before you can smelt metal. It needs no fuel to *exist* — only to *burn* — and it bakes a whole family of goods a vanilla furnace never touches.

!!! warning "Research Kilns first"
    Until you complete the **Kilns** research (it sits just past [Knapping](knapping.md) on the [tech tree](../civilization/research-and-eras.md)), a Clay Ball won't stick — you'll see *"Research Kilns before you can clay cobblestone."*

### Step 1 — Clay your cobblestone

Place ordinary **Cobblestone** blocks where you want to work, then, holding a **Clay Ball**, right-click each one. The stone is packed with wet clay and becomes **Clayed Cobblestone**.

You need **eight** in total for one kiln.

### Step 2 — Stack the cube

Arrange the eight Clayed Cobblestone into a solid **2×2×2 cube**. The instant the cube is complete it collapses in on itself and forms a **Kiln** — no crafting grid, no menu.

Note which way it settles: **the kiln's mouth faces you**. That mouth is where you'll load and unload, so build it where you can reach the front.

!!! tip "No door, no fuss"
    A Kiln has no hinged door like the [Bloomery](metalworking.md). You load it, light it, let it burn out, and collect — that's the whole rhythm.

---

## Firing the Kiln

Once built, the Kiln runs on a simple four-beat loop.

- [ ] **Load** — right-click the mouth with the raw material you want to fire. Add several stacks; the kiln happily bakes a batch.
- [ ] **Light** — strike the mouth with **Flint & Steel** to set the fire. (The early-game **Fire Sticks** you used to light your first campfire work here too.)
- [ ] **Stoke** — feed **Coal** or **Charcoal** into the mouth to keep the flames alive while the batch bakes.
- [ ] **Collect** — when the fire dies down, right-click the mouth **empty-handed** to pull out the finished goods.

!!! success "Full yield, no hunger"
    Kiln firing bakes clay, ceramics, and lime **without the fuel appetite of a furnace** — and every recipe here returns its result at **100% yield**. Nothing is lost to the fire.

### What the Kiln bakes

Feed it raw clay, unfired ceramics, wood, or stone, and it returns hardened goods. Firing times are short but vary by piece — heavier vessels take longer to cure through.

| Put in | Get out | Firing time |
|---|---|---|
| Any Logs | **Charcoal** | ~5s |
| Clay Ball | **Brick** | ~5s |
| Unfired tool mold | Fired tool mold | ~5s |
| **Raw Mud Brick** | Mud Bricks | ~4s |
| **Unfired Clay Pot** | **Fired Clay Pot** | ~6s |
| Clay (block) | **Terracotta** | ~7s |
| **Clay Bucket** | **Fired Clay Bucket** | ~7s |
| **Unfired Crucible** | **Crucible** | ~9s |
| **Unfired Clay Tank** | **Clay Tank** | ~10s |
| **Limestone** | **Quicklime** | ~10s |

That last row is the reason the Kiln matters beyond pottery — it's your only source of lime. More on that below.

!!! tip "Charcoal makes itself"
    Since the Kiln chars **logs into Charcoal**, it can produce the very fuel it burns. Fire a batch of wood first and you'll never run dry stoking the next one.

## The Pottery Slab

The Kiln bakes clay hard — but first something has to *shape* it. That's the **Pottery Slab**: a low working surface where you throw soft clay into pots, buckets, crucibles, and molds.

!!! warning "Research Pottery first"
    **Pottery** is a separate research that sits *behind* Kilns on the tech tree — you need the Kiln before you can unlock the slab. Until you do, a Clayed Cobblestone won't pack down and you'll see *"Research Pottery before you can shape a Pottery Slab."*

### Form the slab

Place a single **Clayed Cobblestone** where you want to work. Holding at least **2 Clay Balls**, right-click it. The clay is packed down over the stone and it becomes a **Pottery Slab** in place.

### Shape the clay

Now the fun part. Place a block of **Clay** on the slab and choose the piece you want to form — since every vessel starts from the same lump, you pick which one you're throwing. Then **hold left-click and spin your mouse** in a circle, as if working a wheel. Keep spinning until the piece is done and it lifts off as a finished (but unfired) vessel.

!!! info "Pottery is a no-pressure craft"
    Unlike [knapping](knapping.md) or [metalworking](metalworking.md), pottery has **no quality minigame** — there's no timing, no grade, no failed piece. Complete the spins and you get a clean result every time. The only "difficulty" is how many spins a shape takes.

Different vessels ask for the same lump of clay but a different number of turns — and some come off the wheel several at a time:

| Shape it into | Clay used | Spins | Yield |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Raw Mud Brick** | 1 Clay block | 2 | 4 |
| **Clay Mold** (base) | 1 Clay block | 3 | 4 |
| **Unfired Clay Pot** | 1 Clay block | 3 | 1 |
| **Clay Bucket** | 1 Clay block | 4 | 1 |
| **Unfired Crucible** | 1 Clay block | 5 | 1 |

Everything you throw here comes off the slab **unfired** and soft. Carry the batch to your Kiln and bake it hard before it's good for anything.

## Ceramic goods

Here's what all that shaping and firing actually gets you.

=== "Pots & buckets"

    **Fired Clay Pots** are your settlement's first purpose-made storage vessels — durable, cheap, and made entirely without metal.

    **Fired Clay Buckets** behave like a bucket once baked: dip one to fill a **Fired Clay Water Bucket** or scoop a **Fired Clay Lava Bucket**. This is your earliest way to move liquids by hand, well before iron pails.

    !!! warning "Fire before you fill"
        A raw **Clay Bucket** or **Unfired Clay Pot** is just soft mud — it won't hold water and it'll slump. Always harden it in the Kiln first.

=== "The Crucible"

    The **Crucible** is the single most important vessel you'll fire here. Throw an **Unfired Crucible** on the slab (five spins), bake it in the Kiln, and you have the pot that carries molten metal.

    In [Metalworking](metalworking.md) you drop raw ore into it, heat it inside a lit **Bloomery** until it runs molten, then carry it to a Stone Anvil and pour it into a mold to cast a tool head. Charge copper and tin together and it alloys **bronze**.

    !!! tip "It cools between pours"
        A crucible that sits too long goes cold — *"the crucible has cooled — reheat it in a bloomery."* Melt and pour in one trip when you can.

=== "Clay molds"

    A **Clay Mold** (base) is the blank you shape into a casting mold — the slab throws them **four at a time**. Right-click a base with a **wood or stone tool of the shape you want** — an axe, pickaxe, sword, knife, hammer, chisel, hoe, shovel, spear, arrow, or plain ingot — to imprint it. That gives an *unfired* shaped mold; fire it in the Kiln to set the impression permanently.

    The finished mold is what your molten crucible pours into. See [Metalworking](metalworking.md) for casting, and [Fletching & Archery](fletching-and-archery.md) for the arrow and spear molds.

=== "Clay Tank"

    The **Clay Tank** is a liquid reservoir for the [Tannery](tannery.md). Unlike the vessels above, its **Unfired Clay Tank** isn't thrown on the wheel — you craft it on a [Crafting Stone](knapping.md) from **four blocks of Clay**, then fire it in the Kiln.

    Set a finished tank beside a tanning rack; **each one adds eight buckets of capacity** for holding water and curing liquid while you process hides. Stack more tanks to hold more.

## Lime & plaster

The Kiln does one thing no other station can: it bakes **stone into lime**.

Feed a piece of **Limestone** into the mouth and it fires down to **Quicklime** — a caustic white powder that opens up two important chains:

- **Curing liquid** for the [Tannery](tannery.md). Fill a Clay Tank with water, stir in Quicklime, and it becomes the curing bath that turns scraped hides into leather.
- **Plaster** for finishing and [building](building.md). The Pottery research also unlocks **Plaster** and a **Paint Brush**, the binder and tool behind smooth lime-plastered walls.

!!! example "From cliffside to leather"
    1. Mine **Limestone** from exposed rock.
    2. Fire it in the **Kiln** → **Quicklime**.
    3. Fill a **Clay Tank** with water, add the Quicklime → **curing liquid**.
    4. Dip a scraped hide to cure it in the [Tannery](tannery.md).

    One resource, three stations, and a raw hide becomes leather.

Limestone also cuts into **Limestone Bricks** and their stair, slab, and wall variants for handsome pale masonry — see [Building Materials](building.md) and [Masonry](masonry.md).

## Salt & the Block of Salt

**Salt** isn't fired on the slab, but it belongs to the same practical age of preservation. Once you complete the **Food Preservation** research, salt becomes your simplest way to make the harvest last.

To salt food, **hold Salt in one hand and a perishable food in the other, then hold use**. The food gains a **Salted** tag in its tooltip and **lasts 25% longer** before it spoils. A **Block of Salt** is the compact way to stockpile it.

See [Cooking & Food](cooking-and-food.md) for the wider preservation picture — salting, drying, and smoking against leaner days.

## The Potter

Once your settlement grows, you don't have to throw every pot by hand. Run a Pottery Slab as a **tier-2 workshop** and assign a **Potter** — a citizen who works the slab, shaping your stored clay into vessels, bricks, and crucibles on order while you tend to bigger things.

See [Workshops](../civilization/workshops.md) for setting up a working station, and [Jobs & Labor](../civilization/jobs-and-labor.md) for assigning citizens to the craft.

!!! quote "Mastering the kiln"
    *"The kiln is the gateway to ceramics and tanning. Mastering it is the step between gathering and true industry."*
