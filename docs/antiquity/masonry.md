# Masonry

<p class="bb-lead"><em>Masonry is to stone what carpentry is to wood. Set a budget of rough rock, pick your cuts from a floating preview, and run a single chisel-strike to dress the whole pile into building blocks.</em></p>

!!! abstract "TL;DR"
    - Research **Masonry** at the Campfire, then **knap a Stone Chisel** from two rocks.
    - Place **two Stone or Cobblestone blocks side by side** and right-click one with the chisel to form a **Mason's Bench** (two blocks long).
    - **Stack base stone** on the bench to set your budget (sneak adds a whole stack).
    - A **floating preview** shows the cuts — slabs, stairs, walls, bricks, polished. Click to queue.
    - **Right-click with the Stone Chisel** and play the **strike minigame** to dress the whole batch.
    - No quality here — masonry is about **throughput**, not craftsmanship.

Masonry is what lets a settlement build in stone: durable walls, stairs, foundations and polished facing. It branches off [Knapping](knapping.md), running in parallel with the kiln-and-[pottery](pottery.md) line, and it turns the piles of cobble and quarried rock you accumulate into proper dressed blocks — in bulk.

## Research it, then knap the tool

Masonry is a learned craft. Head to your settlement **Campfire** and research **Masonry** (see [Research & Eras](../civilization/research-and-eras.md)). Until you do, the chisel recipe won't shape.

Once it's researched, make the mason's signature tool — the **Stone Chisel**. Like every early tool, you [knap](knapping.md) it: bring **two rocks** together and shape the chisel outline by hand. The Stone Chisel is the key to everything on this page — it forms the bench, and it's the tool you swing in the strike minigame.

!!! note "One tool, three jobs"
    The Stone Chisel is used to **form** the Mason's Bench, to **operate** it (the strike step), and — kept in a workshop's storage — to **staff** a Masonry workshop so a Mason can run it for you.

## Build the Mason's Bench

The bench isn't crafted in a grid — you **lash it up in the world**:

- [ ] Place **two Stone or Cobblestone blocks side by side**.
- [ ] Hold your **Stone Chisel** and **right-click one of the two blocks**.

Both blocks fuse into a **Mason's Bench**, which is **two blocks long** — place one half and the other appears. Forming it **costs the chisel some durability**, so don't be surprised to see the tool wear a little.

!!! tip "Site it where the stone is"
    Because you'll be feeding raw stone into the bench and pulling dressed blocks back out, build it next to your quarry, your cobble stockpile, or wherever a road can reach — you'll be shuttling stacks in and out.

## Batch stone-working

This is the heart of masonry. The bench doesn't do one block at a time — you set a **budget**, queue the **cuts** you want, and dress the entire pile in one go.

### 1. Set your budget

Right-click the bench with **base stone** in hand to stack it onto the bench. The amount you load is your **budget** — the pool of rough rock the batch will draw from.

- Right-click adds stone a bit at a time.
- **Sneak + right-click** adds a **whole stack** at once.

The bench accepts the seven **stone families** below as budget material.

### 2. Browse the floating preview

With stone on the bench, a **floating ghost preview** appears above it, cycling through the cuts that stone can become — **slabs, stairs, walls, bricks and polished blocks**. **Browse** the preview and **click to queue** the cut you want. Queue as many different outputs as your budget will cover; the readout tracks your **Queue** and lets you **Remove** entries you change your mind about.

!!! warning "Queue something first"
    If you try to strike with nothing queued, the bench tells you *"Queue an output first (click the floating preview)."* Always pick at least one cut before you start chiseling.

### 3. Play the chisel-strike

When the queue is set, **right-click the bench with your Stone Chisel** to begin the **strike minigame** (the bench calls it *Chiseling*). A marker sweeps across a bar; **click (or press ++space++) as the marker crosses the centre** to land the strike. Clear the strike and the bench dresses the **whole batch** at once, spending your stone budget and dropping the finished blocks.

!!! info "Throughput, not craftsmanship"
    Unlike carpentry or smithing, **masonry has no quality tier**. A dressed block is a dressed block. The strike step is about **how much** you get through, not how *well* — so masonry is your bulk pipeline for building stone.

## What you can cut

Every dressed block draws from your budget at a flat rate. **Most cuts are one-for-one** with the base stone you feed in, while **slabs are the efficient cut, giving two per stone**.

| Cut | What it's for | Yield per stone |
|-----|---------------|:---------------:|
| **Slab** | Floors, steps, thin layers | **2** |
| **Stairs** | Ramps, roofs, terracing | 1 |
| **Wall** | Fences, battlements, low barriers | 1 |
| **Bricks** | The classic dressed-stone look | 1 |
| **Polished** | Smooth, refined facing | 1 |

Some stones also take **cut**, **smooth** and **chiseled** forms for extra decorative range — sandstone most of all, plus smooth stone and chiseled stone bricks. These are one-for-one too.

### The stone families

The bench works seven fixed families of rock. Feed any of these in as your budget:

- **Cobblestone** and **Stone**
- **Sandstone** and **Red Sandstone**
- **Andesite**, **Diorite** and **Granite**

!!! warning "Not every stone makes every cut"
    Vanilla's stone matrix is **irregular** — for example there's no such thing as a *stone wall* or *cobblestone bricks*, and "polished" only exists for a few stones (and is a whole different set of blocks). The floating preview only ever shows you the cuts that **actually exist** for the stone you loaded, so if a family is missing a cut you expected, that block simply doesn't exist in the game. (Deepslate, tuff and blackstone aren't dressed here — they're not Antiquity stone.)

??? note "Reference: which cuts each family supports"
    The bench offers exactly the vanilla blocks that exist for a stone, so the menu is different from family to family. In-game the floating preview is the source of truth, but here's the shape of it:

    | Family | Slab | Stairs | Wall | Bricks | Polished | Smooth | Cut | Chiseled |
    |--------|:----:|:------:|:----:|:------:|:--------:|:------:|:---:|:--------:|
    | **Cobblestone** | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | — | — | — | — | — |
    | **Stone** | ✅ | ✅ | — | ✅ | — | ✅ | — | ✅ |
    | **Sandstone** | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | — | — | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
    | **Red Sandstone** | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | — | — | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
    | **Andesite** | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | — | ✅ | — | — | — |
    | **Diorite** | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | — | ✅ | — | — | — |
    | **Granite** | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | — | ✅ | — | — | — |

    Stone's "bricks" is Stone Bricks and its "chiseled" is Chiseled Stone Bricks; the sandstones carry the richest decorative set.

!!! example "A batch of stone bricks"
    You've got a chest of cobblestone and a fresh quarry of stone. You:

    1. Sneak-right-click the bench with **stone** to dump a full stack onto the budget.
    2. Click the floating preview until it shows **stone bricks**, and click to queue.
    3. Right-click with your **Stone Chisel**, nail the strike as the marker hits centre.
    4. The bench spends the stone and stacks up **stone bricks** — your walls just got a face-lift.

## Two ways to dress stone

Once you've built the bench, you can run masonry yourself or hand it off to a citizen. They dress the same blocks at the same rate — the difference is whether *you* swing the chisel.

=== "By hand"

    You stand at the bench, load a budget, queue cuts, and play the strike minigame yourself. Fast, direct, and great early on when you only need a stack or two of a specific block for the build in front of you.

=== "By a Mason"

    A citizen assigned to the **Mason** job runs a Masonry workshop for you, pulling base stone from storage and dressing it into whatever you've ordered — no minigame, no babysitting. This is how you keep builders supplied at settlement scale.

## Let a Mason do the chiseling

Once your settlement is running, you shouldn't be swinging a chisel by hand forever. Build a **Masonry [workshop](../civilization/workshops.md)** and assign a citizen to the **Mason** job — they *"work a masonry workshop, chiseling stocked stone into slabs, stairs, walls, bricks and polished blocks."*

For the workshop to operate, keep a **Stone Chisel in its storage**. Just as the carpenter's saw lives at the bench rather than in the worker's hands, the mason's chisel stays in the workshop — the Crafter who staffs it works tool-free. Stock the workshop with base stone, order the cuts you want, and your Mason keeps the building blocks flowing while you get on with founding the next town.

!!! tip "Feed the pipeline"
    A Mason only cuts what you stock. Point your haulers at the workshop with cobblestone, stone and sandstone, set **minimum-stock** levels or standing **orders** for the blocks your builders need, and the workshop pulls in just enough raw rock to keep up.

!!! warning "No chisel, no work"
    If the workshop's storage doesn't hold a Stone Chisel, the Mason simply can't work — the workshop reports a missing tool. Keep a spare chisel on hand so a worn-out one doesn't stall the whole line.

## Sharper chisels

The Stone Chisel is only the beginning. Once you've learned [Metalworking](metalworking.md), you can **cast metal chisels** — pour molten copper, tin or bronze into a **Chisel Mold** to produce a **Copper**, **Tin** or **Bronze Chisel**. These follow the same [tool tiers](tool-tiers.md) as the rest of your kit: tougher, longer-lasting, and a smoother upgrade for a mason who's dressing stone all day.
