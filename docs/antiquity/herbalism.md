# Herbalism & Poisons

<p class="bb-lead"><em>Every leaf is medicine or murder in the right hands. Learn to grind, and the same forest that heals you can also drop a fleeing deer, tip an arrow, or lace a rival's supper.</em></p>

!!! abstract "The short version"
    - Research **Herbalism** to unlock the herbs and the ability to grind.
    - Craft a **Mortar & Pestle**, place it, then work the pestle (press down, grind in circles) to turn plants into **dyes, inks, pastes, poisons, and antidotes**.
    - Grind a poison plant into a **paste**, craft it onto a **dart**, and fire it from a **Blowgun**. The same paste can lace food or tip arrows.
    - Each poison has one **antidote** — grown in a *different* biome. No region makes both a poison and its cure, so staying safe means trading, travelling, or conquering.

## Unlocking Herbalism

Herbalism is a research you complete on the way through the ancient era, sitting just past **Foraging**. Finishing it teaches your people the whole herb garden at once — the plants, the poisons, the darts, the Blowgun, the antidotes, and the Mortar & Pestle itself. Mining wild flowers speeds the discovery along.

Until you have it, a Mortar & Pestle will refuse to grind:

!!! warning "Grinding is gated"
    Try to work the pestle before you have the research and the game tells you flatly: *"Research Herbalism before you can grind with a Mortar and Pestle."* You can still craft and place the block early — you just can't use it until the knowledge lands.

See [Research & Eras](../civilization/research-and-eras.md) for how to unlock it, and [Workshops](../civilization/workshops.md) for the wider crafting picture it plugs into.

## The Mortar & Pestle

The **Mortar & Pestle** is the humblest workstation in Antiquity — a stone bowl and a grinding stick — and one of the most versatile. Craft it at your **Crafting Stone** and set it down somewhere you can walk around it.

| Craft | Ingredients | Makes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Mortar & Pestle** | 3 × Cobblestone + 1 × Stick | 1 × Mortar & Pestle |

### Grinding, step by step

Working the mortar is a little hands-on minigame rather than a menu. The rhythm is always the same:

- [ ] **Add water first, if the recipe calls for it.** Right-click the bowl with a **Water Bottle** to pour it in. (Dyes and inks start from water; dry pastes and powders don't.)
- [ ] **Add your ingredient.** Right-click it into the bowl. **Dyes and inks take a single flower**; **pastes and powders grind a whole stack at once**.
- [ ] **Work the pestle.** Right-click the mortar empty-handed to take up the pestle, then **hold left-click to press down and move your mouse in circles** to grind. Release, press again, and repeat.
- [ ] **Finish the batch.** A little counter ticks up — *Crush 1/4, 2/4…* — and **four crushes complete the grind**.
- [ ] **Collect the result.** A finished dye colours items dipped in the bowl (up to eight at a time); pastes, powders, poisons, and antidotes pop straight out.

!!! tip "It's not just poison"
    The same bowl handles everyday colour work — but remember these dyes start from water, so pour a Water Bottle in first. Grind a **poppy** for red dye, **lapis lazuli** for blue, an **allium** for magenta, or **cactus** and **short grass** for green. An **ink sac** — or a **wither rose** — grinds down into black ink. It also crushes fruit like **blueberries** and **sweet berries** into pulp. The Mortar & Pestle is your bridge from raw gathering to prepared goods, feeding kitchens and the [Brewing](brewing.md) chain as much as the poison rack.

!!! question "Where did the water go?"
    Poured water you don't want to use isn't lost — shift-right-click a water-filled mortar with an empty **Glass Bottle** to scoop it back out.

## The herb garden

Herbalism reveals eight plants — four that lead to poisons, four that lead to cures. They aren't sorted into tidy poison-lands and cure-lands: the mountains grow both **wolfsbane** and **arnica**, the jungle both **curare** and **cinchona**, the forest both **nightshade** and **yarrow**. The deliberate rule is subtler — **no biome grows a poison alongside the very antidote that treats it**. Each cure is always somewhere the poison isn't, which turns medicine into a reason to trade and travel (and, sometimes, to fight).

| Herb | Grows in | Leads to |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Wolfsbane** | Mountains | Wolfsbane Poison → darts (paralytic) |
| **Deadly Nightshade** | Forest | Belladonna Poison → Nightshade darts (deliriant) |
| **Curare Vine** | Jungle | Curare Poison → darts (knockout) |
| **Oleander** | Desert | Oleander Poison → darts (cardiac) |
| **Yarrow** | Forest | Yarrow Antidote (cures Wolfsbane) |
| **Marshmallow Root** | Swamp | Marshmallow Antidote (cures Belladonna) |
| **Cinchona** | Jungle | Cinchona Antidote (cures Oleander) |
| **Arnica** | Mountains | Arnica Antidote (cures Curare) |

## Brewing poisons

Grind a poison plant into a paste at the mortar, and you hold the concentrated essence of that leaf. The paste is your raw material for everything that follows — darts, laced food, and tipped arrows.

| Grind this | Into this paste |
| --- | --- |
| Wolfsbane | Wolfsbane Poison |
| Deadly Nightshade | Belladonna Poison |
| Curare Vine | Curare Poison |
| Oleander | Oleander Poison |

Each poison attacks a different part of the body, not just for more damage — so no two feel alike, and each needs its own specific cure.

=== "Wolfsbane — Paralytic"

    The mountain poison. It slows the victim to a crawl, then begins **rooting them in place** with short pulses that pin their feet — the deeper it sinks, the more often the legs give out. Left untreated to the end, the body finally locks up and stops breathing.

    Its death screen reads **"Paralyzed"** — *"Your muscles locked and your breath stilled."*

    !!! tip "A hunter's poison"
        On wild animals, Wolfsbane is pure control: it slows and roots them so they **can't flee**, but never poisons them to death — you still make the kill. It's the difference between a dart and a chase.

=== "Belladonna — Deliriant"

    Ground from **Deadly Nightshade**. It floods the senses with **hallucinations** — warped vision and false sounds — while a slow drain wears the victim down. Poisoned creatures visibly lose their minds, bolting to nowhere and swinging at empty air.

    Its death screen reads **"Lost to Shadows"** — *"You wandered into the nightmares and never returned."*

=== "Curare — Knockout"

    The jungle poison, and the only one that doesn't kill. A brief heavy stagger, then the victim **passes out cold** — fully immobilised and lying down for a spell (longer for animals than players). While they're under, they can be **dragged off with a Fiber Rope**. It's a kidnapper's tool, not a killer's.

    Re-darting an unconscious victim just keeps them under longer, up to a cap — you can't hold someone captive forever with dart-spam alone.

=== "Oleander — Cardiac"

    The desert poison races a clock, not your health bar. It **blocks all healing** the entire time and starts a **fixed cardiac countdown** from the moment it lands — when the timer runs out, the heart simply gives out, no matter the stage. Every extra dose drags the deadline *closer*. The only race is to the antidote.

    Its death screen reads **"Heart Stopped"** — *"Your heart faltered, then gave out."*

!!! note "The fifth poison"
    Beyond the four you can grind and fire, the swamp claims a fifth signature toxin — **Water Hemlock**, a convulsant meant to spread through a crowded settlement. It rounds out the five biome poisons of the cross-biome web, though its own plant and dart aren't yet part of the herb garden.

## Blowguns & darts

A poison is only as good as your ability to deliver it. Craft your paste onto a **dart**, then fire it from a **Blowgun**.

| Craft | Ingredients | Makes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Blowgun** | 3 × Bamboo + 1 × Plant Fiber | 1 × Blowgun |
| **Poison Dart** (e.g. Wolfsbane) | 1 × Bamboo + 1 × Plant Fiber + 1 × matching Poison paste | 4 × Darts |

Any poison's paste makes its own dart, and the Blowgun fires any of them — it simply pulls the first dart it finds in your pack.

### Firing

The Blowgun **draws like a bow**: hold to build your breath, release to puff. A fuller draw sends the dart **faster, straighter, and further**; a snap shot goes wide. It's reusable and spends **one dart per shot**. The dart deals almost no impact damage on its own — the prick is nothing, the poison is everything — and it's never recoverable once thrown.

!!! warning "Hand-throwing is a last resort"
    You *can* fling a dart by hand without a Blowgun, but it's feeble: very short range, wildly inaccurate, and slow to recover from. Treat it as a desperation option, not a delivery system. The Blowgun is the real weapon.

The Blowgun, its darts, and poison-tipped arrows all live alongside your other ranged gear — see [Fletching & Archery](fletching-and-archery.md) for the wider bow-and-arrow craft. The bamboo shafts and plant fibre come from ordinary gathering — cut grass with a **blade or knife** to strip **Plant Fiber**.

## Lacing food & tipping arrows

A poison paste isn't only for darts. Hold the paste in one hand and a **food item** or **any arrow** in the other, then **hold right-click** to rub it on. You coat exactly one item at a time — never the whole stack, so there's no duplication.

=== "Laced food"

    The poison hides **invisibly** inside the food and takes hold a short while after it's eaten — long enough that the victim rarely connects the meal to the collapse. Coat the same food again to **stack the dose**, which starts the poison at a deeper stage. Its tooltip (*"Laced: …"*) shows **only to you and whoever currently shares your settlement** — to everyone else, the food looks perfectly clean.

    !!! danger "Don't stockpile your own poison"
        Laced (and spoiled) food contributes **nothing** to your settlement's food supply, even sitting in claimed storage. A pantry full of poisoned rations is a pantry full of nothing.

=== "Tipped arrows"

    Any arrow works — vanilla or flint alike. A tipped arrow wears its coating **openly** on the tooltip (*"Tipped: …"*), trails the poison's colour in flight, and delivers the toxin to any creature it strikes (on top of the normal arrow damage). One coating, one arrow.

!!! tip "Read the haze"
    A poisoned creature gives off a faint coloured haze in its poison's colour, and darts and tipped arrows trail that same colour mid-air. If you see a purple shimmer around a hunter's quarry or a green streak across the treeline, someone's been busy at the mortar.

## Antidotes & the cure cycle

For every poison there is one leaf that undoes it — ground at the mortar into a remedy. To cure yourself, **drink it like a potion**. To save someone else, **shift-right-click the poisoned creature** — an ally, a citizen, a curare captive — with the matching antidote and it clears them instead. Either way it only answers the one poison it was brewed for; the wrong antidote does nothing.

| Antidote | Ground from | Cures |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Yarrow Antidote** | Yarrow *(Forest)* | Wolfsbane |
| **Marshmallow Antidote** | Marshmallow Root *(Swamp)* | Belladonna |
| **Cinchona Antidote** | Cinchona *(Jungle)* | Oleander |
| **Arnica Antidote** | Arnica *(Mountains)* | Curare |

Notice the geography: **forest yarrow cures mountain wolfsbane**, **swamp marshmallow cures forest belladonna**, **jungle cinchona cures desert oleander**, and **mountain arnica undoes jungle curare** — every cure comes from somewhere its poison doesn't grow. To be ready for every toxin, you need friends (or holdings) across the biomes. Herbalism is as much a [diplomacy](../civilization/diplomacy.md) system as a combat one.

!!! tip "When in doubt, drink milk"
    A bucket of milk is the blunt cure-all: it clears **any** poison at once, no matter which. Keep some for emergencies when you don't have the exact antidote on hand.

!!! example "Curing a curare kidnapping"
    A raider drops one of your citizens with a Curare dart and starts dragging them off by a Fiber Rope. Sprint over and **shift-right-click your captive with the Arnica Antidote** — they snap awake, the rope link drops, and for a short while afterward they **resist fresh curare doses**, so the kidnapper can't simply re-dart them back under.

## Defending against poison

Poison cuts both ways — anything alive can be poisoned, your own scouts and defenders included. An enemy blowgun ambush, whether from a rival civilization or a [barbarian](../civilization/barbarians.md) raid, can paralyse a ranger in the mountains or stop a defender's heart at the desert wall, and the counter is the same one you'd use offensively: keep the matching antidotes stocked, or milk on hand for anything. A settlement that trades for cures across the biomes is one that shrugs off the ambush the poison was meant to win.
