# Woodworking

<p class="bb-lead"><em>The forest is your first quarry. A lone log becomes a stump, a stump becomes a hearth, and a bench of two logs turns a whole armful of timber into planks, tools, and parts in a single pull of the saw.</em></p>

!!! abstract "The short of it"
    - **Chopping Stump** — right-click an **isolated** log with an axe to carve one. Put logs on it, right-click with an axe to split **Firewood** (about half your swings land). Stack four Firewood on the ground to form an unlit **Campfire**.
    - **Woodworking Table** — right-click one of **two adjacent logs** with a **Bone Saw** to form it. It is two blocks long.
    - **Batch woodworking** — right-click logs (and sticks) onto the table to build a **budget** (sneak adds a whole stack), pick a floating preview to **queue** outputs, then right-click with the Bone Saw and work the **sawing minigame** to cut the whole batch at once.
    - Carpentry has **no quality** — it is about throughput, not craftsmanship.

Woodworking is the craft that lets a settlement build up from the dirt. Before it, wood is something you whittle one stick at a time. After it, timber flows: a steady supply of worked wood for tools, housing, and furniture. This page covers the whole chain, from the very first campfire to the mass-production bench that feeds your builders.

---

## The Chopping Stump

Everything starts before you even have a saw. Find or place a **single, isolated log** on the ground, hold an axe, and right-click it. The log is carved into a **Chopping Stump** — a knee-high block that holds logs for splitting.

!!! warning "Isolated means isolated"
    The stump only carves from a log that stands **alone**. Logs still connected to a standing tree are ignored, so you cannot accidentally chew up the trunk you are harvesting. Break a log free, drop it, and place it by itself first.

### Splitting Firewood

Once you have a stump, put logs on it and right-click with an axe to split them. Each swing has a **50% chance** to produce a piece of **Firewood** — so roughly half your strokes land, half glance off. Keep swinging until the log is spent, then collect the Firewood that piles up.

Firewood is your early answer to charcoal: it **replaces charcoal in the earliest campfire crafting**, so you can raise a hearth long before coal is common.

### From Firewood to your first Campfire

You do not need a crafting grid for this — you stack it by hand. Place one piece of **Firewood** on the ground to start a small **Firewood Pile**, then add three more. As the fourth piece goes on, the pile completes into an **unlit Campfire**.

!!! tip "Lighting the fire"
    The Campfire comes out cold. Aim at it with **Fire Sticks** and hold use for about two seconds to spark it alight (Fire Sticks also light an open Bloomery). That lit campfire is more than warmth and a cooking spot — right-click it to open the flow that founds your settlement. See [Founding a Settlement](../getting-started/founding-a-settlement.md).

## The Woodworking Table

The Chopping Stump gets you a fire. The **Woodworking Table** gets you a civilization's worth of lumber. It is the woodwright's bench: you set a pile of wood on it, decide what you want, and saw it all at once.

### Craft a Bone Saw

The tool that builds and runs the bench is the **Bone Saw**. Craft one at a [Crafting Stone](knapping.md) from **2 Bone Blades** and **2 Sticks**: add the parts to the stone, shift-right-click to assemble, then pick up your saw. Bone Blades come from [Knapping](knapping.md), so the saw is a natural next step once you are working flint and bone.

### Form the table

Place **two logs next to each other** on the ground, then right-click one of them with the Bone Saw. The pair snaps together into a Woodworking Table, and the saw loses a little durability in the making.

!!! note "It is two blocks long"
    The table always spans two blocks — place the first half and the second half follows on. Give it clear space, and keep the saw handy: the same Bone Saw that forms the bench is the tool you use to run it.

## Batch woodworking

This is the heart of the craft, and it works differently from a normal crafting grid. Instead of making one item per recipe, you load the bench with raw material, queue up a whole build list, and then cut everything in one go.

### 1. Build a budget

Right-click the table with logs to add them — this pool of wood is your **budget**. For parts that also need sticks (most wooden tools), right-click sticks on as well.

!!! tip "Sneak to load a stack"
    A normal right-click adds one item at a time. **Sneak** and right-click to dump a whole stack onto the bench at once — the fast way to set up a big batch.

### 2. Queue outputs from the floating preview

A **ghost output** floats above the table, showing something your current budget can become. Use the **arrows** to browse everything the wood on the bench could turn into, and **click** a preview to queue it. Every item you queue draws its cost from the budget, so the previews only ever offer what you can still afford. Queue as many as you like, and remove entries you change your mind about.

!!! failure "Nothing to saw?"
    If the bench tells you to *queue an output first*, you have loaded wood but not yet clicked a preview to add it to the build list. Pick something from the floating ghost, then reach for the saw.

### 3. Work the sawing minigame

With a build list set, right-click the table with a **Bone Saw** to begin **Sawing**. **Hold left-click and move your mouse up and down** to draw the saw through the wood. Finish the strokes and the whole batch is cut at once — every queued plank, part, and tool drops together.

!!! quote "No craftsmanship, just throughput"
    The saw is a non-skill step. Unlike knapping or masonry, carpentry has **no quality** roll on its output — a sawn tool is a plain tool. What the bench gives you is volume: dozens of parts from one session at the bench. Later ages can slot a better saw into the same workflow, but the Bone Saw carries you the whole Stone-and-Bronze stretch.

!!! example "A batch, start to finish"
    1. Carry a stack of oak logs, a handful of sticks, and your Bone Saw to the table.
    2. Sneak + right-click to dump the logs onto the bench, then right-click a few sticks on top.
    3. Browse the floating previews — queue **planks** a few times, then a **wooden pickaxe** and a **wooden axe**.
    4. Right-click the table with the Bone Saw and hold left-click, sawing up and down until the strokes finish.
    5. Collect a fresh pile of planks and two new tools — all from one pull of the saw.

## What the bench can make

The Woodworking Table produces two broad kinds of thing: **assembly recipes** (sticks, tools, and parts built from logs and sticks) and **worked wood** (the plank family — planks, stairs, doors, and the rest). Which appear on the bench depends on what you have researched.

### Assembly recipes

These are your day-one workhorses, opened as their recipes unlock. Each is built straight from logs and sticks on the bench.

| Output | Costs | Yield |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Sticks** | 1 log | 4 |
| **Bowl** | 1 log | 4 |
| **Wooden Pickaxe** | 1 log + 2 sticks | 1 |
| **Wooden Axe** | 1 log + 2 sticks | 1 |
| **Wooden Shovel** | 1 log + 2 sticks | 1 |
| **Wooden Hoe** | 1 log + 2 sticks | 1 |
| **Wooden Sword** | 1 log + 1 stick | 1 |
| **Wooden Knife** | 1 log + 1 stick | 1 |
| **Wooden Spear** | 1 log + 3 sticks | 1 |
| **Oar** | 1 log + 2 sticks | 1 |
| **Ladder** | 7 sticks | 3 |
| **Barrel** | 2 logs + 2 sticks | 1 |

!!! tip "Make sticks first, then tools"
    Since sticks come from the same bench (one log for four), a common routine is to queue a batch of sticks, collect them, then load them back on alongside more logs to queue tools. The **Wooden Knife** is worth an early one: more durable than a bone knife, a touch less bite, and it does the same cutting work.

!!! note "The White Banner, too"
    Once Woodworking is researched, the bench can also saw a **White Banner** (6 Thatch + 1 Stick) — your blank canvas for a coat of arms. See [Culture & Heraldry](../civilization/culture-and-heraldry.md).

### Worked wood — the plank trade

Once you research **Carpentry** (an age after basic Woodworking), the full plank trade opens on the same bench. These outputs cover every wood family you feed it, so oak logs make oak planks, spruce logs make spruce stairs, and so on.

| Output | Log cost | Yield |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Planks** | 1 log | 4 |
| **Slab** | 1 log | 8 |
| **Stairs** | 1 log | 4 |
| **Fence** | 1 log | 3 |
| **Sign** | 1 log | 3 |
| **Door** | 1 log | 3 |
| **Trapdoor** | 1 log | 2 |
| **Fence Gate** | 1 log | 1 |
| **Pressure Plate** | 1 log | 1 |
| **Button** | 1 log | 1 |

These are the finished pieces a thriving settlement builds with — see [Building](building.md) for how they fit into houses, workshops, and walls, and [Masonry](masonry.md) for their stone counterparts.

## Let a Woodworker run the bench

You do not have to stand at the saw forever. Once your settlement grows, assign a citizen to the **Woodworker** job and bind a **Woodworking workshop** to a table. The Woodworker withdraws logs and sticks from workshop storage and saws them into whatever you have ordered or set as a minimum stock — planks, parts, and tools — keeping the shelves full hands-free.

Woodworking research also brings in the **Forester**, the citizen who fells trees and hauls logs back to the drop-off (order a plantation to keep the felling local). Between the two, timber cuts itself: the Forester keeps logs coming, the Woodworker turns them into planks and parts, and your builders and downstream workshops draw from the pile. Because carpentry has no quality, the Woodworker's output is identical to your own — pure throughput.

See [Workshops](../civilization/workshops.md) for setting up the building and [Jobs & Labor](../civilization/jobs-and-labor.md) for assigning citizens.

## Research: from stumps to standard chests

Woodworking unfolds across your civilization's ages. Use the tabs below, and see [Research & Eras](../civilization/research-and-eras.md) for the wider tech web.

=== "Woodworking"

    The founding step, reached in the **Ancient** age. It raises the **Woodworking Table** and the **Bone Saw**, brings in the **Woodworker** and **Forester** citizens, and opens the settlement's first worked wood: **sticks**, **wooden tools**, **ladders**, **bowls**, **barrels**, **wooden spears**, **white banners**, and rope- and stick-fences to ring the homestead. This is where the batch bench begins.

=== "Carpentry"

    An age later, in the **Classical** era (once you have reached the Bronze Age). With the saw running true, timber becomes the full **plank trade** — planks, slabs, stairs, doors, trapdoors, fences, gates, signs, pressure plates, and buttons, across every wood family. This is the joinery a proper town is built from, once thatch and rough wood are behind you.

=== "Barrel Making"

    A short **Classical** side-research off Carpentry. Staves drawn tight under wooden hoops turn the barrel from a curiosity into a **sealed cask** — the settlement's first proper vanilla store, raised once the woodwright's bench is running.

=== "Joinery"

    A **Medieval** refinement built on iron working. Iron nails, planed boards, and tight-fitted joints make the **chest** — lockable, stackable, and proof against the weather — the settlement's standard container.
