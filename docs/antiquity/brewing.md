# Brewing

<p class="bb-lead"><em>Leave crushed fruit and water in a hollowed log and the wild yeasts do the rest. Grog is the settlement's first drink — a sour, heady comfort that lifts your people's spirits, doubles as a trade good, and gives you a reason to gather round the trough at the end of a long day.</em></p>

!!! abstract "The short of it"
    - Research **Fermentation** (needs [Herbalism](herbalism.md) first).
    - Pick **Blueberries** or **Sweet Berries** and grind them in a [Mortar & Pestle](herbalism.md) into **Crushed Blueberries** / **Crushed Sweet Berries**.
    - Carve a **Fermentation Trough** into a log with a **Bone Knife**, then right-click it with a **water bucket** to fill it.
    - Right-click the filled trough with the crushed fruit to start it fermenting. Wait about two minutes (a fire nearby speeds it up).
    - Drink straight from the trough, or fill a **Mug** or **Drinking Horn** to carry it. Your citizens will wander over for a sip on their own — and come away happier.

## Why brew at all

Grog is the earliest thing your settlement makes purely for morale. An idle citizen who finds a full trough in your claimed land will break off, wander over, and take a drink — and walk away with the thought *"I had a good drink."* Keep a batch or two flowing and your people are measurably happier for it.

It is more than a mood-lifter, though. Grog stores and travels, so it makes a fine early **trade good**, and the same fermenting know-how becomes the base for steeping medicine later. For a stone-age people, a hollowed log full of sour berry-wine is a small luxury worth celebrating.

## Step 1 — Gather fermentables

Grog starts with fruit. Two work out of the box:

- **Blueberries** — picked from wild **Blueberry Bushes**, which grow in forests and plains. Right-click a ripe bush to pull a handful of berries; unlike sweet-berry bushes these are friendly and won't prick you for reaching in. Blueberries replant and bonemeal just like their vanilla cousins, and make a decent snack on their own.
- **Sweet Berries** — the ordinary vanilla bush berries.

Neither goes into the trough whole. You first have to **crush** them.

!!! note "Crushing at the Mortar & Pestle"
    Drop the berries into a **Mortar & Pestle** and right-click it empty-handed to work the pestle — press down, then grind in circles. A few crushes finish the batch and turn the fruit into a paste:

    | Fruit | Crushed into |
    | --- | --- |
    | Blueberries | **Crushed Blueberries** |
    | Sweet Berries | **Crushed Sweet Berries** |

    Grinding needs the **Herbalism** research (the same one that unlocks the mortar). See [Herbalism](herbalism.md) for the full grinding walkthrough.

## Step 2 — Carve a Fermentation Trough

The vessel is a **Fermentation Trough** — a shallow, hollowed-log tub. Point at any **log** and right-click it with a **Bone Knife** to carve the trough in place. There's a matching trough for every common wood — oak, spruce, birch, jungle, acacia, dark oak, mangrove, and cherry — so the tub always matches the tree it came from.

??? tip "Line them up into a bigger pool"
    Set troughs of the **same wood, all facing the same way** side by side and they join into one shared pool, up to **three troughs** long. A bigger pool holds more liquid — which means more servings per batch — and the whole run ferments and serves as a single batch.

    Keep going past three and the line simply splits into back-to-back pools of three, each with its own liquid and its own timer. Break a trough out of the middle and the two halves re-group on their own.

### Filling it with water

Once carved, the trough needs water before it can ferment. You have three ways to fill it:

| Method | How | Fills |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Bucket** | Right-click with a **water bucket** (a vanilla iron bucket or the era's fired-clay water bucket) | One whole trough at a time — a three-trough pool takes three buckets |
| **Hand-scoop** | Empty-handed, right-click a trough sitting within a few blocks of a stream or pond | A splash at a time; repeat to top it up |
| **Rain** | Leave it open to the sky | A slow trickle, even away from any water source — the tub catches rain like a cauldron |

!!! warning "Fill it before you charge it"
    Once a pool is fermenting or holding finished grog, it won't take any more water. Top it up to the level you want **before** you add the fruit — a fuller trough at charge time means more servings out of that batch.

## Step 3 — Start it fermenting

With water in the tub, right-click the trough holding your **crushed fruit**. The mash goes in, the surface starts to bubble, and fermentation begins. From here you can walk away — the trough keeps its own timer.

Fermenting takes about two minutes. When the batch is ready you'll hear a soft chime and see a little puff of vapour rise off the surface — that's your cue it's turned to grog.

!!! tip "Warm it to hurry it along"
    Wild yeast works faster in the warm. Put a **fire, lit campfire, lava, or a magma block** close to the trough — a fire tucked underneath is the classic setup — and the ferment finishes noticeably sooner.

## The grogs

Which crushed fruit you use decides the drink. Both are light, everyday brews that carry a brief buff along with the buzz:

=== "Berry Grog"
    - **From:** Crushed Sweet Berries
    - **Colour:** deep wine-red
    - **Kick:** a brief flush of **Regeneration** with each drink

=== "Blueberry Grog"
    - **From:** Crushed Blueberries
    - **Colour:** dark indigo
    - **Kick:** a short burst of **Haste** with each drink

Both are modest in strength and restore a little food when you drink them. The recipes are data-driven, so a modpack can add more fermentables and grogs; the brewer and troughs pick them up automatically.

## Step 4 — Drink it

A finished pool can be drunk two ways:

- **Straight from the trough.** Right-click the finished pool **empty-handed** to drink communally on the spot. You get the food, the grog's effect, and a measure of tipsiness.
- **From a vessel.** Hold an empty **Mug** or **Drinking Horn** and right-click the pool to fill it, then carry it and drink at your leisure.

Mug
:   A simple wooden cup, unlocked with [Woodworking](woodworking.md).

Drinking Horn
:   A hollowed goat's horn, unlocked with Animal Husbandry.

Filling a vessel draws one **serving** from the pool and stamps the cup or horn with that grog. Drink it later with a right-click, and you're left holding the empty vessel again, ready to refill. Drinking straight from the trough works the same way — one right-click, one serving gone.

Each serving drains one portion from the pool. When the last serving is gone the grog is finished and the trough resets to empty, ready for your next batch.

!!! example "A first batch, start to finish"
    1. Research **Fermentation** and forage a stack of **Sweet Berries**.
    2. Grind them at a **Mortar & Pestle** into **Crushed Sweet Berries**.
    3. Fell a tree and carve one of its logs with a **Bone Knife** — you now have a **Fermentation Trough**.
    4. Right-click the trough with a **water bucket** to fill it.
    5. Right-click it again holding the **Crushed Sweet Berries**. The surface bubbles: it's fermenting.
    6. (Optional) Slide a lit campfire underneath to speed things along.
    7. Two minutes later a chime and a puff of vapour say it's ready. Right-click empty-handed for a communal sip — or fill a **Mug** to carry a round back to camp.

!!! warning "Pace yourself"
    Grog gets you drunk, and sips taken close together **stack**. As the intoxication climbs the world starts to swim, you stumble, and eventually your controls turn against you; drink past your limit and you **black out cold**, coming to groggy a while later. Very drunk and you'll start throwing up (which costs hunger, not health), and sleeping it off while still hammered earns you a **hangover** in the morning. Worst of all for a busy settlement: **drunk or hungover hands botch their work** — anything you [knap](knapping.md), [fletch](fletching-and-archery.md), or hammer while impaired comes out crude. Save the heavy drinking for after the day's crafting is done.

## Let your citizens enjoy it

You don't have to hand out drinks. Any off-shift citizen within your **claimed territory** who spots a trough of finished grog will wander over, take a serving, and come away with a small lift in mood — the *"I had a good drink."* thought — plus a moment of tipsy wobble for flavour. It's an occasional treat, not a constant habit, and children skip it. No one heads to the trough during a [crisis](../civilization/crises.md), though, so keep the good times for peacetime.

The practical takeaway: a trough or two of standing grog inside your claims is a quiet, steady happiness boost for the whole settlement. Keep it topped up and your people keep smiling.

## The Brewer and the Brewery

Once you've researched Fermentation you can hand the whole cycle off to a crafter. Designate a **Brewery** workshop containing a **Mortar & Pestle** and one or more **Fermentation Trough** pools, and a **Brewer** will keep it running end to end:

- [ ] **Pestle** raw berries into crushed fruit at the mortar
- [ ] **Fill** the troughs with water hand-scooped from a nearby source
- [ ] **Charge** each empty pool with a crushed batch to start it fermenting

After that the trough's own timer runs while the brewer moves on, and citizens serve themselves — so grog is never an item you order or collect; it's simply *available* in the pool. The brewer keeps every pool producing. Crushed Blueberries and Crushed Sweet Berries **are** ordinary craftable goods, so you can stockpile them as trade stock or set a min-stock on them.

!!! tip "Site the brewery by water"
    The brewer fills troughs by scooping from a nearby water source, so place your Brewery beside a stream or pond. A rain-fed pool can still brew, but a proper water source keeps every batch as full as possible — and fuller batches mean more servings. See [Workshops](../civilization/workshops.md) for how to lay out and staff a workshop.

## Research

Two techs sit behind the brewing chain:

| Research | Era | Unlocks |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Fermentation** | Ancient | The Fermentation Troughs (all woods), Crushed Blueberries & Crushed Sweet Berries, and the **Brewer** crafter. Needs [Herbalism](herbalism.md) first. |
| **Barrel Making** | Classical | The sealed wooden barrel — staves drawn tight under hoops for a proper store. A [Carpentry](woodworking.md)-age follow-up, raised once the woodwright's bench is running. |

Fermentation is the one you need to brew at all; Barrel Making comes much later and is really about proper storage rather than the drink itself.
