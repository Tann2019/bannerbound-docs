# The Antiquity Age

<p class="bb-lead">Vanilla hands you a crafting table and an iron age in an afternoon. Antiquity does not. Here, every material is earned: you chip flint against stone, weave fiber into rope, fire clay in a kiln you built brick by brick, and pour molten bronze into a mould you shaped and baked yourself.</p>

The **Antiquity** expansion replaces vanilla's early progression with a tactile, hands-on tech tree that spans the **Bone Age**, the **Stone Age**, and the **Bronze Age**. Most stations use small skill-based minigames — knapping, sawing, chiseling, spinning clay, hammering metal — where your timing sets the **quality** of what you make.

!!! tip "This is the tech; that is the civilization"

    Antiquity is *what* you build; the [Civilization](../civilization/index.md) layer is *who* builds it. Early on you make these things by hand. As your settlement grows, you research the crafts and hand the stations over to specialist **crafters** — Woodworkers, Masons, Potters, Smiths, Tanners, Cooks, and Brewers — who work them in [workshops](../civilization/workshops.md) for you.
