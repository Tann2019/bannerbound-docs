# Knapping & First Tools

<p class="bb-lead">Before any furnace, before a single plank is sawn, your tools are bone and stone. You make them by hand — striking flint, cutting grass, and piling raw materials onto a stone you carve yourself. This is the working edge of the bone age, and everything downstream begins here.</p>

!!! abstract "The short version"
    - **Right-click a hard stone surface** while holding **Gravel** to flake off **Flint**, then knap that Flint into a **Flint Blade**. Bone knaps into two **Bone Blades**.
    - **Break grass, ferns, and leaves** with any blade or knife to gather **Plant Fiber** and **Sticks**.
    - Have no cobblestone? Combine **4 Stone Rocks** in your inventory grid to bootstrap one.
    - **Carve a Crafting Stone** by right-clicking cobblestone with a Flint Blade — your first workbench.
    - **Pile loose materials** on the Crafting Stone, watch the spinning preview, then **sneak + right-click** to knap them together.
    - **Bone tools** assemble straight from blades, sticks, and fiber — no research required.
    - **Stone tools** need a **head** you shape in a knapping minigame: hold a **Stone Rock in each hand** and right-click (after the Knapping research). A mistimed strike can shatter the stone.

## Strike stone against stone

You begin with nothing but the land, and the earliest edge you can make is **Flint**. Hold a block of **Gravel** and **right-click a hard stone surface** — natural stone, cobblestone, or any solid rock face. Each strike consumes the gravel, and roughly one smash in four flakes off a usable piece of flint. Keep a stack of gravel handy; the rest of it just crumbles away.

Once you have flint, knap it the same way. Hold the **Flint** and right-click a hard stone surface again, and it chips into a **Flint Blade** — your first cutting edge. Bone works too: right-click stone with a plain **Bone** and it splits into **two Bone Blades** at once, a cheaper (if flimsier) source of edges.

| You hold… | Action | Result | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Gravel** | Right-click a hard stone surface | **Flint** | 25% chance per strike; consumes the gravel |
| **Flint** | Right-click a hard stone surface | **Flint Blade** | Your first true cutting edge |
| **Bone** | Right-click a hard stone surface | **2 Bone Blades** | Cheap, disposable edges |

!!! tip "Any hard stone will do"
    You don't need a special block to knap against — a natural stone wall, an exposed boulder, or a placed cobblestone all count. If nothing happens, you're probably aiming at dirt, sand, or a soft surface. Find rock.

!!! info "Two different meanings of 'knapping'"
    This surface-flaking — smashing gravel, flint, and bone against rock to pop off blades — is the *simple* knapping you do from your very first minute. It is **not** the same as the timing minigame further down this page, which shapes **stone tool heads** and only opens once you've earned the **Knapping** research. Blades first; heads much later.

## Cutting: fiber and sticks

A blade is not just for tools — it's how you harvest the soft materials every early recipe wants. Hold a **blade or knife** and break plants with it:

| Break this with a blade/knife | You may get | Chance |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Grass or ferns** | **Plant Fiber** | ~50% |
| **Leaves** | **Sticks** | ~40% |

**Plant Fiber** is the twine of the stone age — it binds tool heads to their handles and twists into rope and string later on. **Sticks** are your handles and shafts. Clear a meadow and a few leaf blocks with a blade in hand and you'll have a starter pile of both in short order.

!!! warning "Bare hands won't cut it"
    Punching grass and leaves the vanilla way does **not** yield fiber or sticks here — you must have a cutting edge in hand. This is exactly why the flint blade is your very first craft: nothing else in the toolkit opens up until you can cut.

## Bootstrapping cobblestone

Your first real workbench, the Crafting Stone, is carved out of a cobblestone block — but what if you have no cobblestone and no pick to mine any? The world provides. Small **Stone Rocks** lie scattered loose across the ground; you walk right through them and they pop free the instant you touch them. Gather **four Stone Rocks** and combine them in your **2×2 inventory crafting grid** to press out a single **Cobblestone**. Sandstone and red sandstone rocks work the same way for their matching blocks.

That cobblestone is all you need to begin.

!!! tip "Stone Rocks do double duty"
    Don't burn through every rock you find. **Stone Rocks — and their sandstone cousins — are also the raw material you'll knap into stone tool heads** once you've researched Knapping (see below). Keep a healthy stash in your pack: four rocks is a cobblestone, and every rock beyond that is a swing at a tool head.

??? note "Assemble a Flint Knife in your inventory"
    While you're in the inventory grid, you can also put together a **Flint Knife** — a light weapon that cuts and carves like a blade. Combine a **Flint Blade**, a **Stick**, and a piece of **Plant Fiber** in the 2×2 grid. The knife cuts plants and carves Crafting Stones just like a blade, and it fights better than one — but, unlike a bare blade, it **wears down with use** (about 26 cuts) and will eventually break.

## Carving the Crafting Stone

The **Crafting Stone** is your off-station workbench for the entire bone age: a rough slab where flint and stone become the first tools. To make one, hold a **Flint Blade** (or any knife) and **right-click a cobblestone, sandstone, or red sandstone block**. The block transforms in place into a Crafting Stone, keeping the look of whatever rock you carved it from.

!!! example "From gravel to workbench"
    - [x] Smash gravel on stone until a piece of **Flint** flakes off
    - [x] Knap that flint on stone into a **Flint Blade**
    - [x] Cut grass with the blade for a bit of **Plant Fiber**
    - [x] Gather **4 Stone Rocks** → press a **Cobblestone** in your inventory grid
    - [x] Right-click the cobblestone with the blade → a **Crafting Stone** is carved

## Using the Crafting Stone

The Crafting Stone crafts by **piles, not patterns** — there's no grid to line up. You simply stack the ingredients on top of the stone in any order.

- **Right-click with an item** to place **one** of it onto the stone. Repeat to build up a pile of **up to nine** loose materials, mixing types freely.
- When the pile matches a recipe, a **spinning ghost of the result** floats above the stone as a preview.
- **Sneak + right-click** the stone to knap the pile together — the finished item **pops up off the stone** for you to grab.
- Made a mistake? **Right-click empty-handed** to pull the last item back off the pile.

!!! example "Your first Fire Sticks"
    Fire is the gateway to [founding a settlement](../getting-started/founding-a-settlement.md), and Fire Sticks are the simplest Crafting Stone recipe there is:

    1. Hold a **Stick** and right-click the Crafting Stone. Do it again — **two sticks** now sit on the stone.
    2. A spinning **Fire Sticks** preview appears above it.
    3. **Sneak + right-click** the stone. The Fire Sticks pop off — pick them up.

    From here the recipe list opens wide: **Cobblestone** from four rocks, **Plant String** and **Fiber Rope** from fiber, the bone tools below, and every hafted stone tool all come together on this same slab.

The slab's full menu runs long — for the complete list of piles it accepts, see [every Crafting Stone recipe](../reference/recipes.md#crafting-stone) in the Recipe Reference.

## Bone tools — your first kit

You don't need to wait for any research to arm your camp. Every **bone tool** assembles straight from parts on the **Crafting Stone** — no minigame, no head to shape, just a pile of blades, sticks, and fiber knapped together. This is the working kit that carries you from bare hands to your first fire, first tree, and first hunt.

| Tool | Pile on the Crafting Stone | Good for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Bone Knife** | 1 Bone Blade + 1 Stick + 1 Plant Fiber | Durable everyday cutting; carving Crafting Stones |
| **Bone Axe** | 3 Bone Blades + 2 Sticks + 1 Plant Fiber | Felling trees, carving a Chopping Stump |
| **Bone Pickaxe** | 3 Bone Blades + 2 Sticks + 1 Plant Fiber | Breaking the stone family for drops — but *no ore*, see below |
| **Bone Shovel** | 1 Bone Blade + 2 Sticks + 1 Plant Fiber | Digging dirt, sand, gravel |
| **Bone Hoe** | 2 Bone Blades + 2 Sticks + 1 Plant Fiber | Tilling early farmland |
| **Bone Sword** | 2 Bone Blades + 1 Stick + 1 Plant Fiber | A first real weapon |
| **Bone Spear** | 1 Bone Blade + 3 Sticks + 1 Plant Fiber | Hunting at a safer distance |
| **Bone Club** | 1 Bone + 1 Plant Fiber | The crudest bludgeon — but its crits *stun* (see below) |
| **Bone Saw** | 2 Bone Blades + 1 Stick | Sawing early woodwork |
| **Bone Shears** | 2 Bone Blades + 1 Plant Fiber | Snipping leaves and wool |

!!! warning "A bone pickaxe mines no ore — not even coal or copper"
    Your **Bone Pickaxe** breaks the stone family (stone, cobblestone, and their kin) and gives you the drops, but it pulls **nothing** out of any ore. Swing it at a coal, copper, or iron seam and the block breaks — then drops *nothing at all*. Ore that actually yields something needs a **stone pickaxe**, which is exactly why shaping stone tool heads (below) is the real gate between the bone age and mining. Until then, a bone pick is for digging through rock, not for harvesting metal or coal.

!!! tip "An axe before you can chop"
    Logs can't be punched free in Bannerbound — you need an **axe** to fell a tree at all. A **Bone Axe** is the usual first swing: three bone blades (one and a half bones' worth), a couple of sticks, and a fiber. Fell a tree, drop a lone log on the ground, and right-click it with the axe to carve a **Chopping Stump** for firewood. See [The First Hour](../getting-started/first-hour.md) for the full opening sequence.

!!! tip "Why the Bone Club stuns"
    A blunt weapon is worth carrying for what it does on a **critical hit**: land a crit with the **Bone Club** and the target is *stunned* for a full second — its movement is cut in half, and a struck player's vision blurs. It's a way to stagger something dangerous and get the next few swings in for free. A **rock thrown by hand** carries the same trick: barely any damage, but a brief stun on impact. (The stone hammers you smith later share this blunt stun.)

## Blades versus knives

Every cutting task — harvesting fiber, carving benches, shaping tools — can be done with either a **blade** or a **knife**, but they age very differently.

=== "Blades — a permanent edge"

    **Flint Blades** and **Bone Blades** *never wear out from cutting or carving* — there's no durability bar, so a single blade keeps flaking fiber off grass and carving benches for as long as you hold it. They're quick and cheap to knap straight off a hard stone surface, and Bone Blades are the cheapest of all (two per bone), perfect for the endless grass-cutting and leaf-stripping of your first day. The **only** way to use a blade up is to spend it as an *ingredient* — three bone blades lashed to sticks make a Bone Axe, for instance — so every blade in your pack is either a permanent cutter or future tool-stock.

=== "Knives — they wear down"

    A **knife** does the same cutting and carving, but — unlike a bare blade — it **carries a durability bar and loses a point every time you cut, carve, or strike with it** (a **Flint Knife** lasts about **26 uses** before it breaks). What you get in return is that a knife also **doubles as a light weapon**, with attack reach a bare blade doesn't have. So the reason to carry one isn't that it outlasts your blades — a bare blade is actually the thriftier everyday cutter — it's to keep your **blade stock free for recipes** while still having a proper sidearm in hand.

| Knife | Where you make it | Recipe | Durability |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Flint Knife** | 2×2 inventory grid | Flint Blade + Stick + Plant Fiber | ~26 uses |
| **Bone Knife** | Crafting Stone | Bone Blade + Stick + Plant Fiber | Durable |
| **Wooden Knife** | [Woodworking Table](woodworking.md) | a Log + a Stick | Most durable |

!!! tip "The Wooden Knife is the keeper"
    Once your camp has a [Woodworking Table](woodworking.md), assemble a **Wooden Knife** from a single log and a stick. It's more durable than bone — a touch less bite, but it does exactly the same cutting work and lasts the longest of the three. It's the knife you'll carry for a long while.

## Shaping stone tool heads

Bone tools assemble straight from parts — but **stone tools are different**. Each stone tool needs a **tool head** (an axe head, pick head, shovel head, hoe head, sword blade, or spear point) that you must *shape by hand* in a knapping minigame before you can haft it. This is the true "knapping" the age is named for, and it's the payoff of a research milestone.

!!! warning "Stone heads need the Knapping research"
    The knapping minigame won't open until your settlement has completed the **Knapping** research, which advances you into the **stone tool age** so your workers may equip stone. Try the gesture before then and you'll just be told, *"You haven't learned how to knap stone."* Until you unlock it, lean on your bone tools. See [Research & Eras](../civilization/research-and-eras.md) for how to reach it.

### Opening the minigame

Shaping a head is a **portable hand-craft** — no bench required, you can do it anywhere you're standing. Hold a **Stone Rock in each hand** (main hand *and* off-hand) and **right-click**. Instead of placing a rock, the **knapping screen** opens, offering the tool heads you've unlocked. Sandstone and red sandstone rocks work just as well as plain Stone Rocks.

- Choose a head, then **tap SPACE as each circle hits the ring**. Clean, well-timed strikes shape a better head; a truly **fine** head takes at least one perfectly-timed strike.
- **The first chip consumes one rock.** You knap with two rocks in hand, but each head only costs one — so a pocketful of Stone Rocks is a pocketful of attempts.
- Strike badly and you can **chip the whole stone away**. The game tells you flatly, *"You broke the stone,"* the consumed rock is gone, and you get no head. Steady hands make good tools.

!!! success "Quality carries through"
    The head you knap comes out somewhere between a rough, **crude** edge and a well-struck, **fine** one — and that grade **rides onto the finished tool** when you haft it. Time your strikes well and the same rock yields a noticeably better pickaxe or spear. It pays to slow down.

### Hafting the finished tool

With a head in hand, finish the tool back on the **Crafting Stone**: pile the head together with sticks and plant fiber, then sneak + right-click to knap it whole.

| Finished tool | Head you knap | Haft it with (on the Crafting Stone) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Stone Pickaxe** | Stone Pickaxe Head | + 2 Sticks + 1 Plant Fiber |
| **Stone Axe** | Stone Axe Head | + 2 Sticks + 1 Plant Fiber |
| **Stone Shovel** | Stone Shovel Head | + 2 Sticks + 1 Plant Fiber |
| **Stone Sword** | Stone Sword Blade | + 1 Stick + 1 Plant Fiber |
| **Stone Hoe** | Stone Hoe Head | + 2 Sticks + 2 Plant Fiber |
| **Stone Spear** | Stone Spear Point | + 2 Sticks + 2 Plant Fiber |

!!! info "Heads for the crafts beyond"
    The same minigame also shapes a **Stone Hammer Head** and a **Stone Chisel** — the tools you'll need at the anvil and the mason's bench. A hammer hafts on the Crafting Stone like the others (hammer head + a stick + a fiber). See [Masonry](masonry.md) for dressing stone and [Metalworking](metalworking.md) for cold-hammering metal, where those two earn their keep.
