# Fletching & Archery

<p class="bb-lead">A taut string and a straight shaft put death at arm's reach — and far beyond it. Learn to bind cord to stave, tip a shaft with knapped flint or cast bronze, and hand-fletch a shot true enough to take your quarry from the treeline before it ever knows you are there.</p>

!!! abstract "The short version"
    - **Ranged tools** climb in three steps: the crude **Slingshot** (flings rocks), the **Primitive Bow** (fires arrows), and the poison **Blowgun** (see [Herbalism](herbalism.md)).
    - **Every arrow is three parts** — a **tip**, a **shaft**, and **fletching** — and the combination sets its Damage, Accuracy, and Weight.
    - You build ranged gear at the **Fletching Station**: right-click the parts on like a [Crafting Stone](index.md), then play a short **stretch-timing minigame** that stamps the finished item's **quality**.
    - **Spears** are throwable melee weapons in wood, bone, stone, and metal — and with a rope in your off-hand they become the shallows' oldest fishing tool.
    - Three researches gate it all: **Spear Fishing**, **Fletching**, and **Archery**.

## Getting there: the research

Ranged weapons sit a little way into the [tech tree](../civilization/research-and-eras.md), and the three researches unlock in a line rather than side by side: **Spear Fishing** comes first (it even feeds the cooking branch along the way), then **Fletching** opens the station, and **Archery** sits on top of Fletching.

| Research | Unlocks | Reached via |
|---|---|---|
| **Spear Fishing** | Rope-tethered spear fishing, the **Spear Fisher** job, Plant String & Fiber Rope | after **Hunting** |
| **Fletching** | The **Fletching Station**, the **Slingshot**, the **Workshop Orders** rod, and the **Fletcher** crafter | after **Stew** |
| **Archery** | The **Primitive Bow** and its **Arrows** | after **Fletching** |

!!! tip "Chase the insight"
    Each research quietly rewards the work that leads into it. Gathering a bundle of sticks sparks an **insight** that speeds **Fletching** along, and catching a couple of fish by hand does the same for **Spear Fishing** — do the fieldwork and the knowledge comes faster.

## Early ranged weapons

### The Slingshot

The **Slingshot** is the first thing the [Fletching](#getting-there-the-research) research puts in your hand, and it needs no arrows at all — just a **loose rock** in your inventory (any stone, sandstone, or red-sandstone rock will do). Draw it like a bow: hold to wind the elastic back, release to fling.

A slung rock hits far harder than a bare-handed throw and shatters into the stone's break particles on impact — but a slingshot is honestly a stopgap. It shoots slower, arcs more, and groups looser than a bow, and it wears a point of durability per shot. Draw it to full stretch for the fastest, tightest shot; a barely-pulled release is a dud that fires nothing.

!!! tip "Better-made, steadier"
    A slingshot's [quality](#quality-the-heart-of-the-craft) scales its durability and how tightly it shoots — a Fine sling is noticeably steadier at full draw than a Crude one.

### The Primitive Bow

The **Primitive Bow** waits on the separate **Archery** research. It is a real bow — hold to draw, release to loose — but it is *worse than a vanilla bow by design*: even a Standard-quality bow shoots a touch slower, and a Crude one noticeably slower still. A **Fine** bow, the best your own hands can manage, comes close to a store-bought bow's snap.

The bow's [quality](#quality-the-heart-of-the-craft) scales both **arrow velocity** and **durability**, so a well-fletched bow reaches farther and lasts longer. What it fires, though, is entirely up to the arrow you feed it — and that is where the real depth lives.

### The Blowgun

The **Blowgun** is a bamboo tube that puffs **poison darts** instead of arrows. It draws like a bow — hold to build breath, release to blow — and a longer draw sends the dart faster, straighter, and further. It carries almost no impact of its own; its bite is entirely in the poison it delivers. Darts are brewed from the plants you gather in [Herbalism](herbalism.md), where the blowgun properly belongs.

## Arrows: tip · shaft · fletching

Bannerbound has no fixed list of arrows. Instead, **every arrow is assembled from three interchangeable parts**, and the parts decide everything:

- **Tip** — the killing edge. Sets the arrow's base **damage**.
- **Shaft** — the body. Wood is the standard; a metal shaft adds a little damage but a lot of **weight**.
- **Fletching** — the flights at the back. Sets **accuracy** (how tightly the arrow groups).

Hover any arrow in your inventory and you will see its parts named — *"Flint tip · Wood shaft · Feather fletching"* — above three little bars for **Damage**, **Accuracy**, and **Weight**.

### Tips — the damage

| Tip | Made from | Damage | Weight |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Flint** | a knapped [Flint Blade](knapping.md) | baseline (×1.00) | none |
| **Tin** | a cast Tin Arrowhead | ×1.10 | +1 |
| **Copper** | a cast Copper Arrowhead | ×1.25 | +2 |
| **Bronze** | a cast Bronze Arrowhead | ×1.45 | +3 |

Flint is free and light and will carry you through the whole early game. Metal arrowheads are **cast** in an Arrow Mold at the forge — a job for the [Metalworking](metalworking.md) branch — and each step up the [tier ladder](tool-tiers.md) hits harder.

### Shafts — the weight

| Shaft | Made from | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Wood** | a Stick | weightless — the standard shaft |
| **Tin** | a Tin Ingot | +1 weight, a small damage bump |
| **Copper** | a Copper Ingot | +2 weight, a bigger bump |
| **Bronze** | a Bronze Ingot | +3 weight, the biggest bump |

A metal shaft nudges damage up a few percent per point of weight — but weight is a double-edged thing. **Heavier arrows drop faster:** their arc falls away sooner, so they carry less far and you'll have to aim higher at range. You are trading reach for a slightly harder punch. Most of the time a plain **Wood shaft** is the right call; save metal shafts for close, heavy hits.

### Fletching — the accuracy

| Fletching | Made from | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Feather** | a Feather | tight flight — the most accurate |
| **Fiber** | Plant Fiber | looser flight, but cheap and always to hand |

**Feathers fly true**; raw **Fiber** scatters a little. Fiber's virtue is that you never run out of it — when the feathers are gone, a fiber-fletched arrow still puts something downrange.

!!! example "Reading the combos"
    - **Flint · Wood · Feather** — the honest starter arrow: light, accurate, cheap, and endlessly available.
    - **Bronze · Wood · Feather** — the marksman's arrow: hardest-hitting tip, still light and dead accurate.
    - **Bronze · Bronze · Fiber** — a brutal short-range slug: maximum punch, but heavy (short range) and loose (poor grouping). A specialist's shot, not an everyday one.

!!! tip "Poison the tip"
    Arrows can be **tipped with poison**, exactly like a blowgun dart — a tipped arrow's tooltip reads *"Tipped: …"*. The poisons come from [Herbalism](herbalism.md).

!!! warning "Unknown ammunition"
    An arrow is only usable if your civilization **recognizes all three of its materials**. Pick up a stray arrow tipped with a metal you haven't researched yet and it reads as *"You don't recognize this ammunition"* — you can't fire it until you've unlocked its parts. Loot from other cultures may sit useless in your quiver until your [research](../civilization/research-and-eras.md) catches up.

## The Fletching Station

Bows, arrows, slings, and rods are not made on a workbench — they are **hand-fletched** at the **Fletching Station**, where a timing minigame turns loose parts into a finished item with a **quality** stamped on it.

### Binding a station

Once **Fletching** is researched, **right-click a block of Cobblestone with Plant String**. The cord wraps the stone and it becomes a Fletching Station, ready to work around. (Try it before the research and the string just tells you to *"Research Fletching before you can bind a fletching station."*)

Place it where you have room to stand and work. If you want a citizen to run it, mark a **workshop** around it with the **Workshop Orders** tool that Fletching also unlocks — a station inside a marked workshop becomes a **Fletchery** that your [Fletcher](../civilization/workshops.md) can staff.

### Fletching an arrow, step by step

The station works like the [Crafting Stone](index.md): you build up a **loose pile of ingredients** on it by hand, then commit them.

- [ ] **Right-click the parts onto the station**, one at a time. For a basic arrow that's a **Flint Blade**, a **Stick**, and a **Feather** — tip, shaft, fletching. Swap in any tip, shaft, or fletching you like to make a different arrow.
- [ ] **Sneak + right-click** to commit the pile and begin the **stretch minigame**.
- [ ] **Play the stretch.** A meter fills as you **hold :material-keyboard-space: Space** to draw the string back; **release inside the green zone** to lock in that stretch. Each item takes a few releases in a row, and the green zone **shrinks with every release**, so the later stretches are the tightest.
- [ ] Land the timing and the game calls **"Perfect!"**; a near-miss is **"Good"**; a botched release is a **"Miss."**
- [ ] When the last stretch lands, the **finished arrows are handed straight to you.**

Bows and slingshots are fletched the same way — the difference is only the parts and the number of stretches. A **slingshot** takes two releases; a **bow** and a **fishing rod** take three.

??? note "The Unfinished Primitive Bow"
    When you commit a bow's ingredients (three sticks and three Plant String), the station first hands you an **Unfinished Primitive Bow** and starts the stretch sequence. Complete the stretches and it becomes a finished **Primitive Bow** — the quality you play into those releases is the quality baked into the bow.

### Quality, the heart of the craft

Every fletched item carries a **craftsmanship quality**, and your timing sets it. The ladder runs:

**Crude → Standard → Fine → Masterwork → Perfect → Legendary**

Quality is not cosmetic — it scales the item's real strength: a **bow's** arrow speed and durability, an **arrow's** damage, a **slingshot's** durability, and so on. Better releases push the finished item further up the ladder.

!!! warning "Your hands have a ceiling"
    Hand-crafting at the station **caps out at Fine**. To reach **Masterwork** and beyond you need a skilled **Fletcher** citizen working good equipment — the settlement's first dedicated crafter NPC. Set up a [Fletchery workshop](../civilization/workshops.md) and let a specialist take the craft past what your own hands can manage.

## Spears — thrown weapons

A **spear** is a melee weapon that reaches about **two blocks farther** than a sword *and* can be hurled. Hold right-click to raise it over your shoulder, release to launch it along your look. The thrown spear is the **same spear** — it sticks in whatever it hits, and you pick it back up as the very weapon you threw. Both a melee jab and a throw wear the spear by a point of durability, and a spear worn to its final point **shatters as it leaves your hand** — launched, but gone.

| Spear | Damage | Durability | Made from |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Wooden** | 4.0 | 59 | sticks — the first spear |
| **Bone** | 4.0 | 48 | bone — cheap and quick |
| **Stone** | 5.5 | 131 | a knapped Stone Spear Point |
| **Tin** | 5.0 | 120 | a cast Tin Spear Point |
| **Copper** | 6.0 | 180 | a cast Copper Spear Point |
| **Bronze** | 7.0 | 375 | a cast Bronze Spear Point — the strongest |

All spears share the same reach and swing speed; the tier only changes how hard they hit and how long they last. **Metal spears** are hafted from a cast **spear point** at the [Crafting Stone](index.md) — a [Metalworking](metalworking.md) job. A spear thrown at full wind-up arcs down sooner than an arrow, so it falls short of a bow's range — think of it as a heavy, recoverable close-range shot.

!!! tip "Arming your defenders"
    Spears and bows aren't just for you. Hand them to your settlement's [Guards](../civilization/jobs-and-labor.md) and your defenders can strike from range — a wall lined with archers turns back a [barbarian](../civilization/barbarians.md) raid far more cheaply than swords in the open.

## Spear-fishing

The oldest harvest of all: a spear, a length of cord, and the fish in the shallows.

Research **Spear Fishing**, put a coil of **Fiber Rope in your off-hand**, and throw any spear at a fish. The rope becomes a tether trailing from the spear, and once it lands you **reel the catch straight back to you — fish and all — without ever wading in.** Miss, and the empty rope hauls the spear home so you never lose it. It is by far the cheapest way to eat while your fields and herds are still maturing.

!!! note "Rope required to reel"
    Throw a spear *without* rope in your off-hand and it behaves like any thrown spear — it'll spear the fish, but you'll have to go and fetch it. The **reel** only happens with **Fiber Rope** off-hand and the **Spear Fishing** research done.

### The Spear Fisher

**Spear Fishing** also unlocks the **Spear Fisher** — a citizen who works the water's edge. Assign one at a marked post and they'll walk the shoreline to a stand beside open water, hunt for a real fish, wind up, and throw a rope-tethered spear, hauling each catch back to their drop-off. They follow the fish: if a spot dries up, they relocate along the bank to wherever fish are biting. A Spear Fisher needs nothing but a spear, which makes them the **cheapest fishing post to set up** — and, because fishing doesn't wait on crops or livestock, one of the cleanest answers to a **[Starvation crisis](../civilization/crises.md)**.

### Spear vs. rod

Your Fletching Station can also fletch a plain **fishing rod**, the seed of the other fishing path. Rod fishing — with its own **Fisher** job, opened by a separate **Fishing** research — is the steadier, higher-yield route: a rod fisher casts and waits, and from a raft in deep water the bites come in far quicker than from the shallows. When you unlock the rod fisher, your spear fishers naturally graduate to it. Both jobs, and how to staff them, live on the [Jobs & Labor](../civilization/jobs-and-labor.md) page.
